          camera crew

         <i>

           Stealth
          </a>
         </i>
         with the crew of the
         <a href="/wiki/Nimitz-class_aircraft_carrier" title="Nimitz-class aircraft carrier">
          Nimitz-class aircraft carrier
         </a>

      <p>
       A
       <b>
        cinematographer
       </b>
       or
       <b>
        director of photography
       </b>
       (sometimes shortened to
       <b>
        DP
       </b>
       or
       <b>
        DOP
       </b>
       ) is the chief over the camera and light
       <a href="/wiki/Film_crew" title="Film crew">
        crews
       </a>
       working on a
       <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">
        film
       </a>
       , television production or other live action piece and is responsible for making artistic and technical decisions related to the image. The study and practice of this field is referred to as
       <a href="/wiki/Cinematography" title="Cinematography">
        cinematography
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The cinematographer selects the camera,
       <a href="/wiki/Film_stock" title="Film stock">
        film stock
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Photographic_lens" title="Photographic lens">
        lenses
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filter_(photography)" title="Filter (photography)">
        filters
       </a>
       , etc., to realize the scene in accordance with the intentions of the
       <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">
        director
       </a>
       . Relations between the cinematographer and director vary; in some instances the director will allow the cinematographer complete independence; in others, the director allows little to none, even going so far as to specify exact camera placement and lens selection. Such a level of involvement is not common once the director and cinematographer have become comfortable with each other; the director will typically convey to the cinematographer what is wanted from a scene visually, and allow the cinematographer latitude in achieving that effect.
      </p>
      <p>
       Several American cinematographers have become directors, including
       <a href="/wiki/Barry_Sonnenfeld" title="Barry Sonnenfeld">
        Barry Sonnenfeld
       </a>
       , originally the
       <a href="/wiki/Coen_brothers" title="Coen brothers">
        Coen brothers
       </a>
       ' DP;
       <a href="/wiki/Jan_de_Bont" title="Jan de Bont">
        Jan de Bont
       </a>
       , cinematographer on films as
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Die_Hard" title="Die Hard">
         Die Hard
        </a>
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Basic_Instinct" title="Basic Instinct">
         Basic Instinct
        </a>
       </i>
       , directed
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Speed_(1994_film)" title="Speed (1994 film)">
         Speed
        </a>
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Twister_(1996_film)" title="Twister (1996 film)">
         Twister
        </a>
       </i>
       . In 2014,
       <a href="/wiki/Wally_Pfister" title="Wally Pfister">
        Wally Pfister
       </a>
       , cinematographer on
       <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Nolan" title="Christopher Nolan">
        Christopher Nolan
       </a>
       's
       <a href="/wiki/Batman_in_film#Christopher_Nolan" title="Batman in film">
        three
        <i>
         Batman
        </i>
        films
       </a>
       , made his directorial debut with
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Transcendence_(2014_film)" title="Transcendence (2014 film)">
         Transcendence
        </a>
       </i>
       ; whilst British cinematographers
       <a href="/wiki/Jack_Cardiff" title="Jack Cardiff">
        Jack Cardiff
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Freddie_Francis" title="Freddie Francis">
        Freddie Francis
       </a>
       regularly moved between the two positions.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">
        History
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cinematographer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Alex_Ferrari.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="957" data-file-width="680" height="310" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Alex_Ferrari.jpg/220px-Alex_Ferrari.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Alex_Ferrari.jpg/330px-Alex_Ferrari.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Alex_Ferrari.jpg/440px-Alex_Ferrari.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Alex_Ferrari.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         A cinematographer on set
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       In the infancy of motion pictures, the cinematographer was usually also the
       <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">
        director
       </a>
       and the person physically handling the camera. As the art form and technology evolved, a separation between director and
       <a href="/wiki/Camera_operator" title="Camera operator">
        camera operator
       </a>
       emerged. With the advent of artificial lighting and faster (more light sensitive)
       <a href="/wiki/Film_stock" title="Film stock">
        film stocks
       </a>
       , in addition to technological advancements in optics, the technical aspects of cinematography necessitated a specialist in that area.
      </p>
      <p>
       Cinematography was key during the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Silent_movie" title="Silent movie">
        silent movie
       </a>
       era; with no sound apart from
       <a href="/wiki/Background_music" title="Background music">
        background music
       </a>
       and no dialogue, the films depended on lighting, acting, and set.
      </p>
      <p>
       In 1919 Hollywood, the then-new motion picture capital of the world, one of the first (and still existing) trade societies was formed: the
       <a href="/wiki/American_Society_of_Cinematographers" title="American Society of Cinematographers">
        American Society of Cinematographers
       </a>
       (ASC), which stood to recognize the cinematographer's contribution to the art and science of motion picture making. Similar trade associations have been established in other countries too.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Societies_and_trade_organizations">
        Societies and trade organizations
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cinematographer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Societies and trade organizations">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       There are a number of national associations of cinematographers which represent members (irrespective of their official titles) and which are dedicated to the advancement of cinematography. These include:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        the
        <a href="/wiki/American_Society_of_Cinematographers" title="American Society of Cinematographers">
         American Society of Cinematographers
        </a>
        (A.S.C.)
       </li>
       <li>
        the
        <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Society_of_Cinematographers" title="Canadian Society of Cinematographers">
         Canadian Society of Cinematographers
        </a>
        (C.S.C.)
       </li>
       <li>
        the
        <a href="/wiki/British_Society_of_Cinematographers" title="British Society of Cinematographers">
         British Society of Cinematographers
        </a>
        (B.S.C.)
       </li>
       <li>
        the
        <a href="/wiki/Australian_Cinematographers_Society" title="Australian Cinematographers Society">
         Australian Cinematographers Society
        </a>
        (A.C.S.)
       </li>
       <li>
        the
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cinematographers_Guild_of_Korea&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cinematographers Guild of Korea (page does not exist)">
         Cinematographers Guild of Korea
        </a>
        (C.G.K.)
       </li>
       <li>
        the
        <a href="/wiki/Filipino_Society_of_Cinematographers" title="Filipino Society of Cinematographers">
         Filipino Society of Cinematographers
        </a>
        (F.S.C.)
       </li>
       <li>
        the
        <a href="/wiki/French_Society_of_Cinematographers" title="French Society of Cinematographers">
         French Society of Cinematographers
        </a>
        (A.F.C.)
       </li>
       <li>
        the
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Italian_Society_of_Cinematographers&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Italian Society of Cinematographers (page does not exist)">
         Italian Society of Cinematographers
        </a>
        (A.I.C.)
       </li>
       <li>
        the
        <a href="/wiki/Indian_cinematographers" title="Indian cinematographers">
         Indian Society of Cinematographers
        </a>
        (I.S.C.)
       </li>
       <li>
        the
        <a href="/wiki/German_Society_of_Cinematographers" title="German Society of Cinematographers">
         German Society of Cinematographers
        </a>
        (BVK)
       </li>
       <li>
        the
        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Spanish_Society_of_Cinematography_Works&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Spanish Society of Cinematography Works (page does not exist)">
         Spanish Society of Cinematography Works
        </a>
        (A.E.C)
       </li>
       <li>
        the European Federation of Cinematographers / IMAGO
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       The A.S.C. defines cinematography as:
      </p>
      <dl>
       <dd>
        A creative and interpretive process that culminates in the authorship of an original work of art rather than the simple recording of a physical event. Cinematography is not a subcategory of photography. Rather, photography is but one craft that the cinematographer uses in addition to other physical, organizational, managerial, interpretive and image-manipulating techniques to effect one coherent process.
        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
         <a href="#cite_note-1">
          [1]
         </a>
        </sup>
       </dd>
      </dl>
