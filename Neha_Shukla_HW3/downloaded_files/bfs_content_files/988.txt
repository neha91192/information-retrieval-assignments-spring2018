      <p>
       An
       <b>
        illustrator
       </b>
       is an
       <a href="/wiki/Artist" title="Artist">
        artist
       </a>
       who specializes in enhancing
       <a href="/wiki/Writing" title="Writing">
        writing
       </a>
       or elucidating concepts by providing a visual representation that corresponds to the content of the associated text or idea. The
       <a href="/wiki/Illustration" title="Illustration">
        illustration
       </a>
       may be intended to clarify complicated concepts or objects that are difficult to describe textually, which is the reason illustrations are often found in children's books.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Illustration is the art of making images that work with something and add to it
       <b>
        without needing direct attention
       </b>
       and without distracting from what they illustrate. The other thing is the focus of the attention, and the illustration's role is to add personality and character without competing with that other thing.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Illustrations have been used in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Advertisement" title="Advertisement">
        advertisements
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Architectural_rendering" title="Architectural rendering">
        architectural rendering
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Greeting_card" title="Greeting card">
        greeting cards
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Poster" title="Poster">
        posters
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Book" title="Book">
        books
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Graphic_novels" title="Graphic novels">
        graphic novels
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Storyboards" title="Storyboards">
        storyboards
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/User_guide" title="User guide">
        manuals
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Business" title="Business">
        business
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Magazine" title="Magazine">
        magazines
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shirts" title="Shirts">
        shirts
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Video_games" title="Video games">
        video games
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tutorials" title="Tutorials">
        tutorials
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Newspapers" title="Newspapers">
        newspapers
       </a>
       . A
       <a href="/wiki/Cartoon" title="Cartoon">
        cartoon illustration
       </a>
       can add humor to stories or essays.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Techniques">
        Techniques
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Illustrator&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Techniques">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Simmons-Hermia_and_Lysander._A_Midsummer_Night%27s_Dream.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="4000" data-file-width="3379" height="260" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Simmons-Hermia_and_Lysander._A_Midsummer_Night%27s_Dream.jpg/220px-Simmons-Hermia_and_Lysander._A_Midsummer_Night%27s_Dream.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Simmons-Hermia_and_Lysander._A_Midsummer_Night%27s_Dream.jpg/330px-Simmons-Hermia_and_Lysander._A_Midsummer_Night%27s_Dream.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a4/Simmons-Hermia_and_Lysander._A_Midsummer_Night%27s_Dream.jpg/440px-Simmons-Hermia_and_Lysander._A_Midsummer_Night%27s_Dream.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Simmons-Hermia_and_Lysander._A_Midsummer_Night%27s_Dream.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         A watercolor, by
         <a href="/wiki/John_Simmons_(painter)" title="John Simmons (painter)">
          John Simmons
         </a>
         , depicting
         <i>
          Hermia and Lysander, from
         </i>
         A Midsummer Night's Dream
         <i>
          (1870).
         </i>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Use reference images to create scenes and characters. This can be as simple as looking at an image to inspire your artwork, or creating character sketches and detailed scenes from different angles to create the basis of a picture book world. Some traditional illustration techniques include
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Watercolor" title="Watercolor">
        watercolor
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pen_and_ink" title="Pen and ink">
        pen and ink
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Airbrush" title="Airbrush">
        airbrush
       </a>
       art,
       <a href="/wiki/Oil_painting" title="Oil painting">
        oil painting
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Pastel" title="Pastel">
        pastels
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Wood_engraving" title="Wood engraving">
        wood engraving
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Linocut" title="Linocut">
        linoleum cuts
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_Held,_Jr." title="John Held, Jr.">
        John Held, Jr.
       </a>
       was an illustrator who worked in a variety of styles and media, including linoleum cuts, pen and ink drawings, magazine cover paintings, cartoons,
       <a href="/wiki/Comic_strip" title="Comic strip">
        comic strips
       </a>
       , and set design, while also creating fine art with his animal sculptures and watercolor, many established illustrators attended an art school or college of some sort and were trained in different painting and drawing techniques.
      </p>
      <p>
       Traditional illustration seems to have made a resurgence in the age of social media thanks to social networks like Instagram, Facebook, Pinterest, and YouTube. Currently traditional and digital illustration are both flourishing.
      </p>
      <p>
       Universities and art schools offer specific courses in illustration (for example in the UK, a BA (Hons)
       <a href="/wiki/Academic_degree" title="Academic degree">
        Degree
       </a>
       ) so this has become a new avenue into the profession. Many illustrators are
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freelance" title="Freelance">
        freelance
       </a>
       , commissioned by publishers (of newspapers, books, or magazines) or advertising agencies. Most
       <a href="/wiki/Scientific_visualization" title="Scientific visualization">
        scientific illustrations
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Technical_illustration" title="Technical illustration">
        technical illustrations
       </a>
       are also known as
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Information_graphics" title="Information graphics">
        information graphics
       </a>
       . Among the information graphics specialists are medical illustrators who illustrate human anatomy, often requiring many years of artistic and medical training.
      </p>
      <p>
       A particularly popular medium with illustrators of the 1950s and 1960s was
       <a href="/wiki/Casein_paint" title="Casein paint">
        casein
       </a>
       , as was egg
       <a href="/wiki/Tempera" title="Tempera">
        tempera
       </a>
       . The immediacy and durability of these media suited illustration's demands well. The artwork in both types of paint withstood the rigors of travel to clients and printers without damage.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Digital_illustration">
        Digital illustration
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Illustrator&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Digital illustration">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Rina_Resting_Comfortably_With_Blue_Cats.png">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="694" data-file-width="1230" height="124" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Rina_Resting_Comfortably_With_Blue_Cats.png/220px-Rina_Resting_Comfortably_With_Blue_Cats.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Rina_Resting_Comfortably_With_Blue_Cats.png/330px-Rina_Resting_Comfortably_With_Blue_Cats.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Rina_Resting_Comfortably_With_Blue_Cats.png/440px-Rina_Resting_Comfortably_With_Blue_Cats.png 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Rina_Resting_Comfortably_With_Blue_Cats.png" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Illustrator Rina Zeniuk Utilizes Photoshop Software for her Blue Cats Series (2017)
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Digital illustration is the technique of using a computer to produce original artwork. Digital illustrators use a combination of illustration software and image editing software to create computer art. Digital illustration is not merely the manipulation of images with software; it is the actual creation of new art with digital tools.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Computer illustration, or digital illustration, is the use of digital tools to produce images under the direct manipulation of the artist, usually through a pointing device, such as a tablet or a mouse.
      </p>
      <p>
       Computers dramatically changed the industry and today, many cartoonists and illustrators create
       <a href="/wiki/Digital_illustration" title="Digital illustration">
        digital illustrations
       </a>
       using computers,
       <a href="/wiki/Graphics_tablet" title="Graphics tablet">
        graphics tablets
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Image_scanner" title="Image scanner">
        scanners
       </a>
       . Software such as
       <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Illustrator" title="Adobe Illustrator">
        Adobe Illustrator
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Adobe_Photoshop" title="Adobe Photoshop">
        Adobe Photoshop
       </a>
       , Corel Painter, and Affinity Designer are now widely used by those professionals.
      </p>
