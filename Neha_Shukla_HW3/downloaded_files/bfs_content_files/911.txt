      <p>
       A
       <b>
        film release
       </b>
       is the authorization by the owner of a completed
       <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">
        film
       </a>
       to a public exhibition of the film. The exhibition may be in theatres or for home viewing. A film's
       <b>
        release date
       </b>
       and the method of release is part of the
       <a href="/wiki/Marketing" title="Marketing">
        marketing
       </a>
       of the film. It may be a
       <a href="/wiki/Wide_release" title="Wide release">
        wide
       </a>
       or
       <a href="/wiki/Limited_release" title="Limited release">
        limited release
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The process may involve finding a
       <a href="/wiki/Film_distributor" title="Film distributor">
        film distributor
       </a>
       . A film's marketing may involve the film being shown at a
       <a href="/wiki/Film_festival" title="Film festival">
        film festival
       </a>
       or
       <a href="/wiki/Trade_fair" title="Trade fair">
        trade show
       </a>
       to attract distributor attention and, if successful, may then be released through a chosen distributor.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Delayed_release">
        Delayed release
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Art_release&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Delayed release">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       A
       <b>
        delayed release
       </b>
       or
       <b>
        late release
       </b>
       in the
       <a href="/wiki/Film_industry" title="Film industry">
        film industry
       </a>
       refers to the relatively late
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Film_release" title="Film release">
        release
       </a>
       of a film to the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/General_public" title="General public">
        public
       </a>
       . A release can be postponed due to the sometimes difficult transition of the
       <a href="/wiki/Filmmaking#Production" title="Filmmaking">
        production
       </a>
       or
       <a href="/wiki/Filmmaking#Post-production" title="Filmmaking">
        post-production
       </a>
       to the
       <a href="/wiki/Filmmaking#Distribution_and_exhibition" title="Filmmaking">
        sales and distribution
       </a>
       phase of the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Film_production#Stages_of_Filmmaking" title="Film production">
        film production cycle
       </a>
       . Due to several factors a film release can be delayed:
      </p>
      <ol>
       <li>
        Problems during post-production of an artistic nature.
       </li>
       <li>
        Political problems regarding the film.
       </li>
       <li>
        Economic problems relating to limitations in the
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Film_budget" title="Film budget">
         film budget
        </a>
        .
       </li>
      </ol>
      <p>
       These problems can be resolved by overcoming artistic problems, making politically correct or commercially successful changes to the film/or relieving budgetary problems.
      </p>

      <p>
       In the
       <a href="/wiki/Music_industry" title="Music industry">
        music industry
       </a>
       , a
       <b>
        release
       </b>
       usually is a creative output from an artist, available for sale or distribution; a broad term covering the many different formats music can be released in, and different forms of pieces (singles, albums, extended plays, etc.).
      </p>
      <p>
       The word can also refer to the event at which an
       <a href="/wiki/Album" title="Album">
        album
       </a>
       or
       <a href="/wiki/Single_(music)" title="Single (music)">
        single
       </a>
       is first offered for sale in record stores. Also an
       <b>
        album launch
       </b>
       , or
       <b>
        single launch
       </b>
       .
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2015)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Musical performers often
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Self-release" title="Self-release">
        self-release
       </a>
       (self-publish) their recordings without the involvement of an established
       <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">
        record label
       </a>
       . While some acts who enjoy local or small scale popularity have started their own labels in order to release their music through stores, others simply sell the music directly to customers, for example, making it available to those at their live concerts. With the growth of the Internet as a medium for publicizing and distributing music, many musical acts have sold their recordings over the Internet without a label. Unlike self-publishing a novel, which is usually done only when no other options exist, even well-established musicians will choose to self-release recordings. Music managers are increasingly getting involved in such releases and with the advent of artist management labels which have stepped in to save the situation.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify">
          <span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (January 2017)">
           clarification needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       In Kenya, for example, most record labels only handle production, thus leading to a situation where records are marketed less. This has prompted music companies like
       <a href="/wiki/Grosspool_Music" title="Grosspool Music">
        Grosspool Music
       </a>
       to sign independent artists and manage their branding, releases, and marketing.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">

           citation needed
