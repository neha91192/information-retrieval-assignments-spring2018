   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Executive producer
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       <b>
        Executive producer
       </b>
       (
       <b>
        EP
       </b>
       ) is one of the top positions in the making of a commercial
       <a href="/wiki/Entertainment" title="Entertainment">
        entertainment
       </a>
       product. Depending on the
       <a href="/wiki/Media_(communication)" title="Media (communication)">
        medium
       </a>
       , the executive producer may be concerned with
       <a href="/wiki/Management_accounting" title="Management accounting">
        management accounting
       </a>
       or with associated legal issues (like
       <a href="/wiki/Copyright" title="Copyright">
        copyrights
       </a>
       or
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Royalties" title="Royalties">
        royalties
       </a>
       ).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imdb_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-imdb-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In films, the executive producer generally contributes to the
       <a href="/wiki/Film_budgeting" title="Film budgeting">
        film's budget
       </a>
       and usually does not work on set, in contrast to most other
       <a href="/wiki/Film_producer" title="Film producer">
        producers
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Motion_pictures">
        Motion pictures
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Executive_producer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Motion pictures">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Film_producer" title="Film producer">
        Film producer
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       In films, an executive producer
       <a href="/wiki/Film_finance" title="Film finance">
        finances
       </a>
       the film or participates in the creative effort, but does not work on the set. His/her responsibilities vary from
       <a href="/wiki/Funding" title="Funding">
        funding
       </a>
       or attracting investors into the movie project to
       <a href="/wiki/Law" title="Law">
        legal
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Screenplay" title="Screenplay">
        scripting
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Marketing" title="Marketing">
        marketing
       </a>
       , advisory and supervising capacities.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The crediting of executive producers in the film industry has risen over time. In the mid-to-late 1990s, there were an average of just under two executive producers per film. In 2000, the number jumped to 2.5 (more than the number of standard "
       <a href="/wiki/Film_producer" title="Film producer">
        producers
       </a>
       "). In 2013, there were an average of 4.4 executive producers per film, compared with 3.2 producers.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Television">
        Television
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Executive_producer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Television">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Television_producer" title="Television producer">
        Television producer
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       In television, an executive producer usually supervises the creative content and the financial aspects of a production. Some writers, like
       <a href="/wiki/Stephen_J._Cannell" title="Stephen J. Cannell">
        Stephen J. Cannell
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Tina_Fey" title="Tina Fey">
        Tina Fey
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Ryan_Murphy_(writer)" title="Ryan Murphy (writer)">
        Ryan Murphy
       </a>
       , have worked as both the creator and the producer of the same TV show.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In case of multiple executive producers on a TV show, the one outranking the others is called the
       <a href="/wiki/Showrunner" title="Showrunner">
        showrunner
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       or the leading executive producer.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Music">
        Music
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Executive_producer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Music">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       See also:
       <a href="/wiki/Music_executive" title="Music executive">
        Music executive
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       In recorded music,
       <a href="/wiki/Record_label" title="Record label">
        record labels
       </a>
       distinguish between an executive producer and a
       <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">
        record producer
       </a>
       . The executive producer is responsible for business decisions and more recently, organizing the recordings along with the music producer, whereas the record producer produces the music. Sometimes the executive producer organises the recording and selects recording-related crew, such as
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sound_engineer" title="Sound engineer">
        sound engineers
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Session_musician" title="Session musician">
        session musicians
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Discovery_Channel_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Discovery_Channel-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Video_games">
        Video games
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Executive_producer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Video games">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Video_game_producer" title="Video game producer">
        Video game producer
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       In the
       <a href="/wiki/Video_game_industry" title="Video game industry">
        video game industry
       </a>
       , the title "executive producer" is not well-defined. It may refer to an external producer working for the
       <a href="/wiki/Video_game_publisher" title="Video game publisher">
        publisher
       </a>
       , who works with the
       <a href="/wiki/Video_game_developer" title="Video game developer">
        developers
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       For example, in 2012,
       <a href="/wiki/Jay-Z" title="Jay-Z">
        Jay-Z
       </a>
       was announced as executive producer for
       <a href="/wiki/NBA_2K13" title="NBA 2K13">
        NBA 2K13
       </a>
       . His role consisted of appearing in an introduction, picking songs for the game's soundtrack and contributing to the design of its in-game menus "and other visual elements".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
