      <p>
       A
       <b>
        production report
       </b>
       ("PR") is a
       <a href="/wiki/Filmmaking" title="Filmmaking">
        filmmaking
       </a>
       term for the form filled out each day of production of a
       <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">
        movie
       </a>
       or
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_program" title="Television program">
        television show
       </a>
       to summarize what occurred that day. There is no standard template for a production report, and each show usually has an original template, often created before production begins by one of the assistant
       <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">
        directors
       </a>
       ("AD"). Besides superficial differences, most forms record the same information and are simply a series of blank tables created in
       <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Excel" title="Microsoft Excel">
        Excel
       </a>
       printed doublesided on a legal sized (8 x 14 inch) sheet of paper. The purpose of this form is to keep track of a production's progress and expenses and to help determine what salary is owed to the cast and crew. It is finally sent to studio executives and is permanently filed to serve as a legal record.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="The_front">
        The front
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Production_report&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: The front">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The top lists the production title, production company,
       <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">
        director
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Film_producer" title="Film producer">
        producers
       </a>
       , unit production managers, assistant directors, the total number of scheduled production days, and the current production day, day of the week, and date. Below this the PR combines information from several different forms on one page for easy reading:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        The script notes from the Script Supervisor. This details what scenes and pages were completed or were failed to be completed that day, what locations were used, as well as the official lunch time.
       </li>
       <li>
        The
        <a href="/wiki/Movie_camera" title="Movie camera">
         Camera
        </a>
        Report from the Camera Loader. This records how many feet of each different
        <a href="/wiki/Film_stock" title="Film stock">
         film stock
        </a>
        was used and subtracts the total used from the previous day's inventory to determine how much unexposed film the production has left on hand. Other information on this form is the amount of film printed, considered no good, and what amount of short ends (of film stock) was created or used.
       </li>
       <li>
        The Sound Report. This form holds information about what is recorded on each
        <a href="/wiki/Magnetic_tape" title="Magnetic tape">
         sound tape
        </a>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        The Exhibit G from the First Team
        <a href="/wiki/Production_assistant" title="Production assistant">
         production assistant
        </a>
        (PA). This form indicates the work status, in/out times, the meal times, meal penalties, and pick-up/drop-off times of the principal cast.
       </li>
       <li>
        The
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extra_(actor)" title="Extra (actor)">
         Extras
        </a>
        Breakdown from the Background PA. This form records the number of background actors, lists their pay rates, their in/out times, lunch times, meal penalties,
        <a href="/wiki/Overtime" title="Overtime">
         overtime
        </a>
        , and whether they performed any special actions or services that merit
        <a href="/wiki/Merit_pay" title="Merit pay">
         bonus pay
        </a>
        .
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="The_back">
        The back
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Production_report&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The back">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The back lists the names of the entire crew and production office staff separated by department, with each person's in/out times, meal times, and meal penalties. The back also records the number of meals consumed (and thus paid for by the company), any special equipment rented, and has a notes section to mark down any random important information such as details of a theft, fire or accident.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Filling_out_the_report">
        Filling out the report
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Production_report&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Filling out the report">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The report is usually completed by an AD, a Paperwork PA, or a
       <a href="/wiki/Directors_Guild_of_America" title="Directors Guild of America">
        DGA
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Training" title="Training">
        Trainee
       </a>
       . Manpower, crew information, and equipment information is collected from the Key PA, the
       <a href="/wiki/Grip_(job)" title="Grip (job)">
        Best Boy Grip
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best_Boy" title="Best Boy">
        Best Boy Electric
       </a>
       , 2nd Camera Assistant, Assistant Props,
       <a href="/wiki/Scenic_design" title="Scenic design">
        Set Scenic
       </a>
       , Key Hair, and
       <a href="/wiki/Make-up_artist" title="Make-up artist">
        Key Make-Up
       </a>
       during the day. This information can be difficult to gather as it involves finding a moment when these busy crew members are free to talk. The Paperwork PA needs to be perceptive and only interrupt them at a good moment. The number of meals served is counted by the PA during lunch, usually requiring they wait by the buffet line and eat last in order to count how many plates are taken by everyone. The Assistant Directors and Producers will tell the Paperwork PA what notes to add.
      </p>
      <p>
       At the end of the day the Paperwork PA receives the other forms and adds their information to the front. The Paperwork PA will usually drop the PR off at the production office after wrap to be filed, first making a few copies to give to the
       <a href="/wiki/Unit_production_manager" title="Unit production manager">
        unit production manager
       </a>
       (UPM) and Assistant Directors the next day so they can double-check the information and have corrections called into the office if they disagree with any details.
      </p>
