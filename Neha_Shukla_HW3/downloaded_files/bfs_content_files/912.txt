   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Wide release
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div class="noprint" id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div>
    <div id="contentSub">
    </div>
    <div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       In the
       <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_the_United_States" title="Cinema of the United States">
        American motion picture industry
       </a>
       , a
       <b>
        wide release
       </b>
       is a
       <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">
        motion picture
       </a>
       that is playing nationally. This is contrast to a film that is having premiere showings at a few cinemas (usually in
       <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">
        New York
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">
        Los Angeles
       </a>
       ), or is in
       <a href="/wiki/Limited_release" title="Limited release">
        limited release
       </a>
       at selected cinemas in larger cities around the country. Specifically, a movie is considered to be a wide release when it plays in 600 cinemas or more in the United States and
       <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">
        Canada
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In the U.S., films holding an
       <a href="/wiki/Motion_Picture_Association_of_America_film_rating_system#Ratings" title="Motion Picture Association of America film rating system">
        NC-17
       </a>
       rating have almost never received wide releases.
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Showgirls_(film)" title="Showgirls (film)">
         Showgirls
        </a>
       </i>
       (1995) is the only film with an NC-17 rating to have a wide release.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The 1975 film
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Breakout_(1975_film)" title="Breakout (1975 film)">
         Breakout
        </a>
       </i>
       was the first major studio film to go into wide release in its opening week, with
       <a href="/wiki/Columbia_Pictures" title="Columbia Pictures">
        Columbia Pictures
       </a>
       distributing 1300 prints nationwide, combined with a heavy national advertising campaign.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
