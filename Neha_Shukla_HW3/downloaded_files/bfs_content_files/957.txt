      <p>
       A
       <b>
        television director
       </b>
       is in charge of the activities involved in making a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_program" title="Television program">
        television program
       </a>
       , or section of a programme. They are generally responsible for decisions about the editorial content and creative style of a programme, and ensuring the producer's vision is delivered. Their duties may include originating programme ideas, finding contributors, writing scripts, planning 'shoots', ensuring safety, leading the crew on location, directing contributors and presenters, and working with an editor to assemble the final product. The work of a television director can vary widely depending on the nature of the programme, the practices of the production company, and whether the programme content is factual, or drama, and is
       <a href="/wiki/Live_television" title="Live television">
        live
       </a>
       or recorded.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Types_of_Television_Director">
        Types of Television Director
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Television_director&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Types of Television Director">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Factual_Television_Director">
        Factual Television Director
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Television_director&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Factual Television Director">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Factual or documentary TV directors may take any number of roles in the television production process - or combine several roles in one.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Entertainment_Television_Director">
        Entertainment Television Director
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Television_director&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Entertainment Television Director">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       In a
       <a href="/wiki/Television_show" title="Television show">
        television show
       </a>
       composed of individual
       <a href="/wiki/Episode" title="Episode">
        episodes
       </a>
       , the television director's role may differ from a film director's in that he or she will usually work only on some television episodes instead of being the
       <i>
        auteur
       </i>
       of the entire production. In an episodic television production, the major creative control will likely reside with the
       <a href="/wiki/Television_producer" title="Television producer">
        television producer
       </a>
       (s) of the show. However, the director has input, whether it be how, if and why something can or can't be done.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Drama_Television_Director">
        Drama Television Director
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Television_director&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Drama Television Director">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       In a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dramatic_arts" title="Dramatic arts">
        dramatic arts
       </a>
       production, the television director's role can be similar to a
       <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">
        film director
       </a>
       's, giving cues to
       <a href="/wiki/Actor" title="Actor">
        actors
       </a>
       and directing the camera placement and movement.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Live_Television_Director">
        Live Television Director
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Television_director&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Live Television Director">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Primarily, the live director is responsible for "calling" the broadcast, supervising the placement of
       <a href="/wiki/Professional_video_camera" title="Professional video camera">
        professional video cameras
       </a>
       (
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Camera_blocking" title="Camera blocking">
         camera blocking
        </a>
       </i>
       ),
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lighting_equipment" title="Lighting equipment">
        lighting equipment
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Microphone" title="Microphone">
        microphones
       </a>
       , props, graphics and the overall pacing and feel of the production. Other than quickly calling out commands, the television director is also expected to maintain order among the staff in the control room, on the set, and elsewhere.
      </p>
      <p>
       A news studio might have multiple cameras and few camera movements. In a sports broadcast, the director might have 20 or 30 cameras and must continuously tell each of the camera operators what to focus on.
      </p>
      <p>
       While the director is responsible for specific shots and other production elements, the
       <a href="/wiki/Television_producer" title="Television producer">
        producer
       </a>
       (typically seated behind the director in the second row of chairs in the control room) coordinates the "big picture", including commercial breaks and the running length of the show.
      </p>
