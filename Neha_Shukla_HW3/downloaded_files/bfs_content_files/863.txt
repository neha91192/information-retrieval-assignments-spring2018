      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         Passport to Rio
        </b>
       </i>
       (Spanish:
       <i>
        <b>
         Pasaporte a Río
        </b>
       </i>
       ) is a
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Argentine_films_of_1948" title="List of Argentine films of 1948">
        1948
       </a>
       Argentine
       <a href="/wiki/Crime_film" title="Crime film">
        crime film
       </a>
       , directed by
       <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Tinayre" title="Daniel Tinayre">
        Daniel Tinayre
       </a>
       and written by
       <a href="/wiki/C%C3%A9sar_Tiempo" title="César Tiempo">
        César Tiempo
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Luis_Saslavsky" title="Luis Saslavsky">
        Luis Saslavsky
       </a>
       . It premiered on September 29, 1948.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">
        Plot
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Passport_to_Rio&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       A thief made a steal and is observed by a showgirl who becomes witness to the crime. Man discovers and forces to help you take what you stole from
       <a href="/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro" title="Rio de Janeiro">
        Rio de Janeiro
       </a>
       . No other way out, she is shipped to
       <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">
        Brazil
       </a>
       by the thief. On the boat, the showgirl meets a doctor who initiates a love story while the offender is in love with the woman.
      </p>
