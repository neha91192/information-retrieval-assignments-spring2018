   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Property master
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div class="noprint" id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div>
    <div id="contentSub">
    </div>
    <div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       The
       <b>
        property master
       </b>
       , often called the
       <b>
        prop(s) master
       </b>
       , is an
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Artistic" title="Artistic">
        artistic
       </a>
       and organizational employee in a
       <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">
        film
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">
        television
       </a>
       or
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theatrical" title="Theatrical">
        theatrical
       </a>
       production who is responsible for purchasing, acquiring, manufacturing, properly placing, and/or overseeing any
       <a href="/wiki/Theatrical_property" title="Theatrical property">
        props
       </a>
       needed for a production. The property master also works with other members of the production managing the physical appearance of the
       <a href="/wiki/Stage_(theatre)" title="Stage (theatre)">
        stage
       </a>
       or
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Film_set" title="Film set">
        set
       </a>
       , for example they might work with the
       <a href="/wiki/Script_supervisor" title="Script supervisor">
        script supervisor
       </a>
       to maintain set
       <a href="/wiki/Continuity_(fiction)" title="Continuity (fiction)">
        continuity
       </a>
       . The property master is on staff during preproduction, develops the stylistic concept of the physical production, then continues on as a member of the physical shooting/production crew.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       A person responsible for purchasing the props can be called a
       <b>
        props buyer
       </b>
       or
       <b>
        production buyer
       </b>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       During preproduction, the props master develops a props breakdown. This is essentially mapping out the logical progression of each prop throughout the story. During shooting, the props master maintains the logical progression by ensuring the props are positioned in their correct logical place for each scene according to the props breakdown. If the logical progression of a prop changes during shooting, the props breakdown is revised to reflect the change.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       This job description varies somewhat from country to country. In the United States,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Food_styling" title="Food styling">
        food styling
       </a>
       , weapons and animals are often directly or indirectly within the property master's domain.
      </p>
      <p>
       The job is a collaboration with the
       <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">
        director
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Production_designer" title="Production designer">
        production designer
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cameraman" title="Cameraman">
        cameraman
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Set_decorator" title="Set decorator">
        set decorator
       </a>
       and other members of the production to physically express their stylistic and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Aesthetic" title="Aesthetic">
        aesthetic
       </a>
       requirements. They maintain their own budget. There are physical aspects of productions that are managed by specialists other than the property master, for example
       <a href="/wiki/Costume_designer" title="Costume designer">
        costume designers
       </a>
       are responsible for the actors' dress, and
       <a href="/wiki/Weapons_master" title="Weapons master">
        weapons masters
       </a>
       are responsible for any weapons (firearms, blades, staff-based or otherwise).
      </p>
      <p>
       In professional theatre the property master is responsible for the set up and organization of the props. Property master is directed by the director.
      </p>
