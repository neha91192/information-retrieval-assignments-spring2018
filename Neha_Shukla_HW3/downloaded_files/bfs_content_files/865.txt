      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         The Balcony of the Moon
        </b>
       </i>
       (Spanish:
       <i>
        <b>
         El balcón de la Luna
        </b>
       </i>
       ) is a 1962 Spanish
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Musical_comedy_film" title="Musical comedy film">
        musical comedy film
       </a>
       directed by
       <a href="/wiki/Luis_Saslavsky" title="Luis Saslavsky">
        Luis Saslavsky
       </a>
       and starring
       <a href="/wiki/Carmen_Sevilla" title="Carmen Sevilla">
        Carmen Sevilla
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Lola_Flores" title="Lola Flores">
        Lola Flores
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Paquita_Rico" title="Paquita Rico">
        Paquita Rico
       </a>
       . Its critical and commercial failure illustrated the declining popularity of the
       <a href="/wiki/Andalusia" title="Andalusia">
        Andalusian
       </a>
       musical. one of the most popular Spanish genres of the 1950s.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The film's sets were designed by
       <a href="/wiki/Sigfrido_Burmann" title="Sigfrido Burmann">
        Sigfrido Burmann
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>
