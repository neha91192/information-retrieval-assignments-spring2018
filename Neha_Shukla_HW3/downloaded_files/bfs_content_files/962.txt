      <p>
       A
       <b>
        movie star
       </b>
       (also known as a
       <b>
        film star
       </b>
       and
       <b>
        cinema star
       </b>
       ) is an actor who is
       <a href="/wiki/Celebrity" title="Celebrity">
        famous
       </a>
       for their starring, or leading, roles in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Motion_picture" title="Motion picture">
        motion pictures
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The term is used for actors who are marketable stars and whose names are used to promote movies, for example in trailers and posters.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">
        United States
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Movie_star&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: United States">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <h3>
       <span id="Hollywood.27s_early_years">
       </span>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Hollywood's_early_years">
        Hollywood's early years
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Movie_star&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Hollywood's early years">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Less_Than_the_Dust_2.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1283" data-file-width="887" height="318" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Less_Than_the_Dust_2.jpg/220px-Less_Than_the_Dust_2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Less_Than_the_Dust_2.jpg/330px-Less_Than_the_Dust_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Less_Than_the_Dust_2.jpg/440px-Less_Than_the_Dust_2.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Less_Than_the_Dust_2.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Poster advertising a 1916 film with
         <a href="/wiki/Mary_Pickford" title="Mary Pickford">
          Mary Pickford
         </a>
         , one of the first movie stars
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       In the early days of
       <a href="/wiki/Silent_film" title="Silent film">
        silent movies
       </a>
       , the names of the actors and actresses appearing in them were not publicized or credited because producers feared this would result in demands for higher salaries.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-100years_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-100years-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       However, audience curiosity soon undermined this policy. By 1909, actresses such as
       <a href="/wiki/Florence_Lawrence" title="Florence Lawrence">
        Florence Lawrence
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Mary_Pickford" title="Mary Pickford">
        Mary Pickford
       </a>
       were already widely recognized, although the public remained unaware of their names. Lawrence was referred to as the “Biograph Girl” because she worked for
       <a href="/wiki/D._W._Griffith" title="D. W. Griffith">
        D. W. Griffith
       </a>
       's
       <a href="/wiki/Biograph_Studios" title="Biograph Studios">
        Biograph Studios
       </a>
       , while Pickford was "Little Mary." In 1910, Lawrence switched to the
       <a href="/wiki/Independent_Moving_Pictures" title="Independent Moving Pictures">
        Independent Moving Pictures Company
       </a>
       , began appearing under her own name, and was hailed as "America's foremost moving picture star" in IMP literature.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-100years_4-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-100years-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Pickford began appearing under her own name in 1911.
      </p>
      <p>
       The Independent Moving Pictures Company promoted their "picture personalities", including Florence Lawrence and
       <a href="/wiki/King_Baggot" title="King Baggot">
        King Baggot
       </a>
       , by giving them
       <a href="/wiki/Billing_(filmmaking)" title="Billing (filmmaking)">
        billing
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Motion_picture_credits" title="Motion picture credits">
        credits
       </a>
       and a
       <a href="/wiki/Marquee_(sign)" title="Marquee (sign)">
        marquee
       </a>
       .
       <a href="/wiki/Promotion_(marketing)" title="Promotion (marketing)">
        Promotion
       </a>
       in advertising led to the release of stories about these personalities to newspapers and fan magazines as part of a strategy to build brand loyalty for their company's actors and films. By the 1920s, Hollywood film company promoters had developed a "massive industrial enterprise" that "...peddled a new intangible—fame."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Early Hollywood studios tightly controlled who was a movie star, as only they had the ability to place stars' names above the title; according to film historian
       <a href="/wiki/Jeanine_Basinger" title="Jeanine Basinger">
        Jeanine Basinger
       </a>
       , this was done "only for economic reasons".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Hollywood "image makers" and promotional agents planted rumors, selectively released real or fictitious biographical information to the press, and used other gimmicks to create
       <a href="/wiki/Glamour_(presentation)" title="Glamour (presentation)">
        glamorous
       </a>
       personas for actors. Publicists thus "created" the "enduring images" and public perceptions of screen legends such as
       <a href="/wiki/Judy_Garland" title="Judy Garland">
        Judy Garland
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Rock_Hudson" title="Rock Hudson">
        Rock Hudson
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Marilyn_Monroe" title="Marilyn Monroe">
        Marilyn Monroe
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Grace_Kelly" title="Grace Kelly">
        Grace Kelly
       </a>
       . The development of this "
       <a href="/wiki/Star_system_(filmmaking)" title="Star system (filmmaking)">
        star system
       </a>
       " made fame "something that could be fabricated purposely, by the masters of the new 'machinery of glory'."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_5-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       However, regardless of how "...strenuously the star and their media handlers and press agents may ... try to 'monitor' and 'shape' it, the media and the public always play a substantial part in the image-making process."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_5-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       According to Madow, "fame is a 'relational' phenomenon, something that is conferred by others. A person can, within the limits of his natural talents, make himself strong or swift or learned. But he cannot, in this same sense, make himself famous, any more than he can make himself loved."
      </p>
      <p>
       Madow goes on to point out "fame is often conferred or withheld, just as love is, for reasons and on grounds other than 'merit'." According to Sofia Johansson the "canonical texts on stardom" include articles by Boorstin (1971), Alberoni (1972) and Dyer (1979) that examined the "representations of stars and on aspects of the Hollywood star system". Johansson writes that "more recent analyses within media and cultural studies (e.g. Gamson 1994; Marshall 1997; Giles 2000; Turner, Marshall and Bonner 2000; Rojek 2001; Turner 2004) have instead dealt with the idea of a pervasive, contemporary, '
       <a href="/wiki/Celebrity_culture" title="Celebrity culture">
        celebrity culture
       </a>
       '." In the analysis of the celebrity culture, "fame and its constituencies are conceived of as a broader social process, connected to widespread economic, political, technological and cultural developments."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In the 1980s and 1990s, entertainment companies began using stars for a range of publicity tactics including press releases, movie junkets, and community activities. These promotional efforts are targeted and designed using market research, to increase the predictability of success of their media ventures. In some cases, publicity agents may create “provocative advertisements” or make an outrageous public statement to trigger public controversy and thereby generate "free" news coverage.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_5-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Movie studios employed performers under long-term contracts. They developed a star system as a means of promoting and selling their movies. "Star vehicles" were filmed to display the particular talents and appeal of the most popular movie stars of the studio.
      </p>

      <p>
       Movie stars in other regions too have their own star value. For instance, in
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_cinema" title="Asian cinema">
        Asian film
       </a>
       industries, many movies often run on the weight of the star's crowd pulling power more than any other intrinsic aspect of film making.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="China">
        China
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Movie_star&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: China">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       A number of
       <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_China" title="Cinema of China">
        Chinese film
       </a>
       actors have become some of the most popular movie stars in
       <a href="/wiki/East_Asia" title="East Asia">
        Eastern Asia
       </a>
       and are also well known in the Western world. They include
       <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Chan" title="Jackie Chan">
        Jackie Chan
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Jet_Li" title="Jet Li">
        Jet Li
       </a>
       ,

      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Southeast-Asian_archipelago">
        Southeast-Asian archipelago
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Movie_star&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Southeast-Asian archipelago">

        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:AngtangingpamilyaAMARRYGOROUND.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="377" data-file-width="263" height="315" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/46/AngtangingpamilyaAMARRYGOROUND.jpg/220px-AngtangingpamilyaAMARRYGOROUND.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/4/46/AngtangingpamilyaAMARRYGOROUND.jpg 1.5x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:AngtangingpamilyaAMARRYGOROUND.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Poster advertising a 2009 film with
         <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Estrada" title="Joseph Estrada">
          Joseph Estrada
         </a>
         .
         <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
          <a href="#cite_note-22">
           [22]
          </a>
         </sup>

      <p>
       The film industry of the Malay Archipelago (also known as
       <a href="/wiki/Nusantara" title="Nusantara">
        Nusantara
       </a>
       ) consists primarily of film industries in
       <a href="/wiki/Malaysia" title="Malaysia">
        Malaysia
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">
        Indonesia
       </a>
       , the
       <a href="/wiki/Philippines" title="Philippines">
        Philippines
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">
        Singapore
       </a>
       . Over the past century, these four cinemas have collaborated on a number of projects. A number of actors from this region have become some of the most sought-after movie stars in southeast Asia, commonly in
       <a href="/wiki/Malay_language" title="Malay language">
        Malay
       </a>
       -speaking countries.
      </p>
      <p>
       Actors such as
       <a href="/wiki/P._Ramlee" title="P. Ramlee">
        P. Ramlee
       </a>
       , Rano Karno,
       <a href="/wiki/Rima_Melati" title="Rima Melati">
        Rima Melati
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Deddy_Mizwar" title="Deddy Mizwar">
        Deddy Mizwar
       </a>
       , Jins Shamsuddin, Eman Manan, Alex Komang,
       <a href="/wiki/Christine_Hakim" title="Christine Hakim">
        Christine Hakim
       </a>
       , Fauziah Ahmad Daud,
       <a href="/wiki/Nora_Aunor" title="Nora Aunor">
        Nora Aunor
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Joseph_Estrada" title="Joseph Estrada">
        Joseph Estrada
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Padilla,_Jr." title="José Padilla, Jr.">
        Jose Padilla
       </a>
       , Nordin Ahmad,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saadiah" title="Saadiah">
        Saadiah
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Fernando_Poe_Jr." title="Fernando Poe Jr.">
        Fernando Poe Jr.
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Roy_Marten" title="Roy Marten">
        Roy Marten
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Yusof_Haslam" title="Yusof Haslam">
        Yusof Haslam
       </a>
       are considered movie stars of the 20th century, some of them having acted in all four countries.
      </p>

       Other, more recent movie stars include Romalis Syafril,
