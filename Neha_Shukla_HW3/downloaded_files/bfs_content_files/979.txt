   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Production designer
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div class="noprint" id="siteSub">
     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
    </div>
    <div id="contentSub">
    </div>

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       In
       <a href="/wiki/Film_industry" title="Film industry">
        film
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">
        television
       </a>
       , a
       <b>
        production designer
       </b>
       (or
       <b>
        P. D.
       </b>
       ) is the person responsible for the overall visual look of the production. Production designers have a key creative role in the creation of motion pictures and television. Working directly with the
       <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">
        director
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Cinematographer" title="Cinematographer">
        cinematographer
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Film_producer" title="Film producer">
        producer
       </a>
       , they must select the settings and style to visually tell the story. The term
       <i>
        production designer
       </i>
       was coined by
       <a href="/wiki/William_Cameron_Menzies" title="William Cameron Menzies">
        William Cameron Menzies
       </a>
       while he was working on the film
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Gone_with_the_Wind_(film)" title="Gone with the Wind (film)">
         Gone with the Wind
        </a>
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Previously (and often subsequently) the people with the same responsibilities were called "
       <a href="/wiki/Art_director" title="Art director">
        art directors
       </a>
       ".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It is sometimes also described as "
       <a href="/wiki/Scenic_design" title="Scenic design">
        scenic design
       </a>
       " or "set design".
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Societies_and_trade_organizations">
        Societies and trade organizations
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Production_designer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Societies and trade organizations">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In the United States and British Columbia, production designers are represented by
       <a href="/wiki/International_Alliance_of_Theatrical_Stage_Employees" title="International Alliance of Theatrical Stage Employees">
        IATSE
       </a>
       local
       <a class="external text" href="http://www.artdirectors.org/" rel="nofollow">
        800
       </a>
       ; the Art Directors Guild. The production design credit must be requested by the producer, prior to completion of photography, and submitted to the Art Directors Guild Board of Directors for the credit approval. In the rest of Canada, production designers are represented by the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Director%27s_Guild_of_Canada" title="Director's Guild of Canada">
        Director's Guild of Canada
       </a>
       .
      </p>
