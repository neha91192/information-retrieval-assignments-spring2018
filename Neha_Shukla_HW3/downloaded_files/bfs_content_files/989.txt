      <p>
       <b>
        Scenic design
       </b>
       (also known as
       <a href="/wiki/Scenography" title="Scenography">
        scenography
       </a>
       ,
       <b>
        stage design
       </b>
       ,
       <b>
        set design
       </b>
       , or
       <a href="/wiki/Production_designer" title="Production designer">
        production design
       </a>
       ) is the creation of
       <a href="/wiki/Theatre" title="Theatre">
        theatrical
       </a>
       , as well as
       <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">
        film
       </a>
       or
       <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">
        television
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Theatrical_scenery" title="Theatrical scenery">
        scenery
       </a>
       . Scenic designers come from a variety of artistic backgrounds, but in recent years, are mostly trained professionals, holding a
       <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_Fine_Arts" title="Bachelor of Fine Arts">
        B.F.A.
       </a>
       or
       <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Fine_Arts" title="Master of Fine Arts">
        M.F.A.
       </a>
       degrees in theater arts. Scenic designers design
       <a href="/wiki/Theatrical_scenery" title="Theatrical scenery">
        sets
       </a>
       and scenery that aim to support the overal artistic goals of the production.
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Scenic_designer">
        Scenic designer
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Scenic_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Scenic designer">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       A designer looks at the details searching for evidence through research to produce conceptual ideas that’s best toward supporting the content and values with visual elements. The subject of, “How do we generate creative ideas?” is a very legitimate question. The most consuming part of expanding our horizons toward scenic concepts is much more than witnessing creativity, and creative people. It starts with us opening our mind to the possibilities. To have an attitude toward learning, seeking, and engaging in creativity and to be willing to be adventurous, inquisitive and curious. Our imagination is highly visual. Whether outside or inside, colorful trees or concerts, star lit skies or the architecture of a great building, scenic design is a process of discovery. Discovering what will best clarify and support the story being told.
      </p>
      <p>
       The scenic designer works with the director and other designers to establish an overall visual concept for the production and design the stage environment. They are responsible for developing a complete set of design drawings that include the following:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <i>
         basic ground plan
        </i>
        showing all stationary scenic elements;
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         composite ground plan
        </i>
        showing all moving scenic elements, indicating both their onstage and storage positions;
       </li>
       <li>
        section of the stage space incorporating all elements;
       </li>
       <li>
        <i>
         front elevations
        </i>
        of every
        <i>
         scenic element
        </i>
        , and additional elevations or sections of units as required.
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       All of these required drawing elements can be easily created from one accurate 3-D
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/CAD_model" title="CAD model">
        CAD model
       </a>
       of the set design.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Responsibility">
        Responsibility
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Scenic_design&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Responsibility">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:182px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Robert_Edmond_Jones.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3817" data-file-width="2975" height="231" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Robert_Edmond_Jones.jpg/180px-Robert_Edmond_Jones.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Robert_Edmond_Jones.jpg/270px-Robert_Edmond_Jones.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Robert_Edmond_Jones.jpg/360px-Robert_Edmond_Jones.jpg 2x" width="180"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Robert_Edmond_Jones.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Scenic designer
         <a href="/wiki/Robert_Edmond_Jones" title="Robert Edmond Jones">
          Robert Edmond Jones
         </a>
         (1887-1954) drawing at a waist high table (c. 1920).
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       The scenic designer is responsible for collaborating with the
       <a href="/wiki/Theatre_director" title="Theatre director">
        theatre director
       </a>
       and other members of the production design team to create an environment for the production and then communicating the details of this environment to the
       <a href="/wiki/Technical_director" title="Technical director">
        technical director
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Production_manager" title="Production manager">
        production manager
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Charge_artist" title="Charge artist">
        charge scenic artist
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Property_master" title="Property master">
        prop master
       </a>
       . Scenic designers are responsible for creating
       <a href="/wiki/Scale_model" title="Scale model">
        scale models
       </a>
       of the scenery,
       <a href="/wiki/Artistic_rendering" title="Artistic rendering">
        renderings
       </a>
       , paint elevations and scale construction drawings as part of their communication with other production staff.
      </p>
