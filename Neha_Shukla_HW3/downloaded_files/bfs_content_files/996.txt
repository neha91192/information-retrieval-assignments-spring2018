   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Best boy
   </h1>

      <p>
       In a
       <a href="/wiki/Film_crew" title="Film crew">
        film crew
       </a>
       there are two kinds of
       <b>
        best boy
       </b>
       :
       <b>
        best boy electric
       </b>
       and
       <b>
        best boy grip
       </b>
       . They are assistants to their department heads, the
       <a href="/wiki/Gaffer_(filmmaking)" title="Gaffer (filmmaking)">
        gaffer
       </a>
       (in charge of electricals) and the
       <a href="/wiki/Key_grip" title="Key grip">
        key grip
       </a>
       (lighting and rigging), respectively.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Taub153_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Taub153-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In short, the best boy acts as the
       <a href="/wiki/Construction_foreman" title="Construction foreman">
        foreman
       </a>
       for his department. A woman who performs the duties of a best boy may be called
       <b>
        best girl
       </b>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Job_responsibilities">
        Job responsibilities
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Best_boy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Job responsibilities">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Best boys are responsible for the day-to-day operation of the lighting or grip department. Their many responsibilities include the hiring, scheduling, and management of crew; the renting, ordering, inventory, and returning of equipment; workplace safety and maintaining discipline within their department; completing timecards and other paperwork; stocking of expendables; loading and unloading production trucks; planning and implementing the lighting or rigging of locations and/or sound stages; coordinating with rigging crews and additional photography units (if applicable); handling relations with the other production departments; overseeing the application of union rules (where relevant); and serving as the day-to-day representative of the department with the
       <a href="/wiki/Unit_production_manager" title="Unit production manager">
        unit production manager
       </a>
       and coordinator of their department.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The best boy also commonly accompanies or stands in for the key grip or gaffer during technical scouts. During shooting, the best boy may also cover for the key grip or gaffer when he or she is on break or otherwise away from the set.
      </p>
      <p>
       On films with very small crews, the electric (lighting) department often consists of only a gaffer, a best boy, and a few
       <a href="/wiki/Lighting_technician#Film_lighting_technician" title="Lighting technician">
        electricians
       </a>
       . The grip department may include only a key grip, a best boy, and a few
       <a href="/wiki/Grip_(job)" title="Grip (job)">
        grips
       </a>
       . Large-scale productions such as
       <a href="/wiki/Major_film_studio" title="Major film studio">
        major
       </a>
       films commonly include full-time rigging and
       <a href="/wiki/Second_unit" title="Second unit">
        second unit
       </a>
       crews, and in total may hire many dozens of grips or electricians at one time.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Etymology">
        Etymology
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Best_boy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Etymology">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       It's unclear why this term came to be used as a job title on film sets. As OED says, "It has been suggested that it originated as a term for a master's most able apprentice, or alternatively that it was transferred from earlier use for a member of a ship's crew, but confirmatory evidence for either of these theories appears to be lacking." The earliest known appearance of the phrase in print is 1931 from the
       <i>
        Albuquerque Journal
       </i>
       : "Among the electricians .. the department head is the gaffer, his first assistant is the best boy."
      </p>
      <p>
       As the gaffer is sometimes credited as the chief lighting technician, the best boy electric is sometimes credited as the
       <b>
        assistant chief lighting technician
       </b>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       The title is accepted for use in credits by the BBC.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-bbc-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The end credits of the 1980 comedy film
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Airplane!" title="Airplane!">
         Airplane!
        </a>
       </i>
       named the Best Boy, then the next line was "Worst Boy", naming
       <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">
        Adolf Hitler
       </a>
       in that position.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Usage_outside_English">
        Usage outside English
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Best_boy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Usage outside English">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Many
       <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">
        French language
       </a>
       films made in
       <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">
        Canada
       </a>
       use
       <i>
        Best Boy
       </i>
       in the credits. The term has been known to appear in the credits of some French films made in
       <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">
        France
       </a>
       , but it has not been known to appear in
       <a href="/wiki/Belgium" title="Belgium">
        Belgian
       </a>
       or
       <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">
        Swiss
       </a>
       films.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2007)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">
        German
       </a>
       film
       <i>
        Auf der anderen Seite
       </i>
       (
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Edge_of_Heaven_(film)" title="The Edge of Heaven (film)">
         The Edge of Heaven
        </a>
       </i>
       ), which takes place in
       <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">
        Germany
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">
        Turkey
       </a>
       , uses the term
       <i>
        Best Boy
       </i>
       in its German credits. German TV and film crews regularly use the term, because no equivalent phrase exists in German.
      </p>
