   </h1>

         The popular demand for
         <a href="/wiki/Tom_Clancy" title="Tom Clancy">
          Tom Clancy
         </a>
         's action novels exceeded his ability to write new books. As a result, his publisher hired ghostwriters to write novels in the Clancy style.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       A
       <b>
        ghostwriter
       </b>
       is hired to write
       <a href="/wiki/Literature" title="Literature">
        literary
       </a>
       or
       <a href="/wiki/Journalism" title="Journalism">
        journalistic
       </a>
       works,
       <a href="/wiki/Public_speaking" title="Public speaking">
        speeches
       </a>
       or other texts that are officially credited to another person as the
       <a href="/wiki/Author" title="Author">
        author
       </a>
       . Celebrities, executives, participants in timely news stories, and political leaders often hire ghostwriters to draft or edit
       <a href="/wiki/Autobiography" title="Autobiography">
        autobiographies
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Memoir" title="Memoir">
        memoirs
       </a>
       , magazine articles, or other written material. In music, ghostwriters are often used to write songs, lyrics and instrumental pieces. Screenplay authors can also use ghostwriters to either
       <a href="/wiki/Script_doctor" title="Script doctor">
        edit or rewrite their scripts
       </a>
       to improve them.
      </p>
      <p>
       Usually, there is a confidentiality clause in the contract between the ghostwriter and the credited author that obligates the former to remain anonymous. Sometimes the ghostwriter is acknowledged by the author or publisher for his or her writing services, euphemistically called a "researcher" or "research assistant", but often the ghostwriter is not credited.
      </p>
      <p>
       Ghostwriting (or simply "ghosting") also occurs in other creative fields. Composers have long hired ghostwriters to help them to write musical pieces and songs;
       <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_Amadeus_Mozart" title="Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart">
        Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart
       </a>
       is an example of a well-known composer who was paid to ghostwrite music for wealthy patrons. Ghosting also occurs in
       <a href="/wiki/Popular_music" title="Popular music">
        popular music
       </a>
       . A pop music ghostwriter writes lyrics and a melody in the style of the credited musician. In
       <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop" title="Hip hop">
        hip hop
       </a>
       music, the increasing use of ghostwriters by high-profile hip-hop stars has led to controversy.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc.com_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-bbc.com-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In the visual arts, it is not uncommon in either
       <a href="/wiki/Fine_art" title="Fine art">
        fine art
       </a>
       or commercial art such as
       <a href="/wiki/Comics" title="Comics">
        comics
       </a>
       for a number of assistants to do work on a piece that is credited to a single artist. However, when credit is established for the writer, the acknowledgement of their contribution is public domain and the writer in question would not be considered a ghostwriter.
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">
        Overview
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghostwriter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       A consultant or career-switcher may pay a ghostwriter to write a book on a topic in their professional area, to establish or enhance her credibility as an 'expert' in their field. Public officials and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Politicians" title="Politicians">
        politicians
       </a>
       employ "correspondence officers" to respond to the large volume of official
       <a href="/wiki/Communication" title="Communication">
        correspondence
       </a>
       . A number of papal
       <a href="/wiki/Encyclical" title="Encyclical">
        encyclicals
       </a>
       have been written by ghostwriters. A controversial and scientifically unethical practice is medical ghostwriting, where biotech or pharmaceutical companies pay professional writers to produce papers and then recruit (via a payment or as a perk) other scientists or physicians to attach their names to these articles before they are published in medical or scientific journals. Some university and college students hire ghostwriters from
       <a href="/wiki/Essay_mill" title="Essay mill">
        essay mills
       </a>
       to write entrance essays, term papers, theses, and dissertations. This is largely considered unethical unless the actual ghostwriting work is just light editing.
      </p>
      <p>
       Ghostwriters are hired for numerous reasons. In many cases, celebrities or public figures do not have the time, discipline, or writing skills to write and research a several-hundred page autobiography or "how-to" book. Even if a celebrity or public figure has the writing skills to pen a short article, they may not know how to structure and edit a several-hundred page book so that it is captivating and well-paced. In other cases, publishers use ghostwriters to increase the number of books that can be published each year under the name of well-known, highly marketable authors, or to quickly release a topical book that ties in with a recent or upcoming newsworthy event.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Roles">
        Roles
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghostwriter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Roles">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Ghostwriters may have varying degrees of involvement in the production of a finished work. Some ghostwriters are hired to edit and clean up a rough draft or partially completed work, while others are hired to do most of the writing based on an outline provided by the credited author. For some projects, such as creating an autobiography for a celebrity, ghostwriters will do a substantial amount of research. Ghostwriters are also hired to write fiction in the style of an existing author, often as a way of increasing the number of books that can be published by a popular author.
      </p>
      <p>
       Ghostwriters will often spend a period from several months to a full year researching, writing, and editing
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nonfiction" title="Nonfiction">
        nonfiction
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Fiction" title="Fiction">
        fiction
       </a>
       works for a client. Ghostwriters are paid either per page, per each word or via total word count, with a flat fee, with a percentage of the royalties of the sales, or by using some combination thereof.
      </p>
      <p>
       The division of work between the ghostwriter and the credited
       <a href="/wiki/Author" title="Author">
        author
       </a>
       varies a great deal. In some cases, the ghostwriter is hired to edit a rough draft of a mostly completed
       <a href="/wiki/Manuscript" title="Manuscript">
        manuscript
       </a>
       . In this case, the outline, ideas and much of the language in the finished book or article are those of the credited author. If it is agreed upon, for example in a signed contract, the ghostwriter will sign over all the rights to everything he or she adds into the work that is not otherwise copyrighted to someone else. In many cases, a ghostwriter handles most of the writing, using concepts and stories provided by the credited author. In this case, a ghostwriter will do extensive
       <a href="/wiki/Research" title="Research">
        research
       </a>
       on the credited author or their subject area of expertise. It is rare for a ghostwriter to prepare a book or article with
       <i>
        no
       </i>
       input from the credited author; at a minimum, the credited author usually jots down a basic framework of ideas at the outset or provides comments on the ghostwriter's final
       <a href="/wiki/Draft_document" title="Draft document">
        draft
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       For an
       <a href="/wiki/Autobiography" title="Autobiography">
        autobiography
       </a>
       , a ghostwriter will typically interview the credited author, their colleagues, and family members, and find interviews, articles, and
       <a href="/wiki/Video" title="Video">
        video
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Footage" title="Footage">
        footage
       </a>
       about the credited author or their work. For other types of nonfiction books or articles, a ghostwriter will
       <a href="/wiki/Interview" title="Interview">
        interview
       </a>
       the credited author and review previous speeches, articles, and interviews with the credited author, to assimilate his or her arguments and points of view. Most of this work can be done over email via the Internet, through postal mail, phone or video calls, and other methods of instant communication.
      </p>
      <p>
       Also, ghostwriters may work on accompanying documents, such as treatments for screenplays. Often, ghostwriters will work on related projects beyond the scope of professional ghostwriting, such as marketing, promotions, sales, publishing or other related services for pay, in order to procure more clients and increase the total amount of their business.
      </p>

      <p>
       Ghostwriters will often spend from several months to a full year researching, writing, and editing
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nonfiction" title="Nonfiction">
        nonfiction
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Fiction" title="Fiction">
        fiction
       </a>
       works for a client, and they are paid based on a price per hour, per word or per page, with a flat fee, or a percentage of the royalties of the sales, or some combination thereof. Some ghostwriters charge for articles "$4 per word and more depending on the complexity" of the article.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Literary_agent" title="Literary agent">
        Literary agent
       </a>
       Madeleine Morel states that the average ghostwriter's advance for work for major book publishers is "between $15,000 and $75,000".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       These benchmark prices are mirrored approximately in the film industry by the Writer's Guild, where a Minimum Basic Agreement gives a starting price for the screenplay writer of $37,073 (non-original screenplay, no treatment).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       However, the recent shift into the digital age (15–20% world market share of books by 2015) has brought some changes, by opening newer markets that bring their own opportunities for authors and writers
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       —especially on the more affordable side of the ghostwriting business. One such market is the shorter book, best represented at the moment by Amazon's Kindle Singles imprint: texts of 30,000 words and under.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-theguardian.com_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-theguardian.com-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Such a length would have been much harder to sell before digital reader-technologies became widely available, but is now quite acceptable. Writers on the level of Ian McEwan have celebrated this recent change, mainly for artistic reasons.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       As a consequence, the shorter format makes a project potentially more affordable for the client/author. Manhattan Literary, a ghostwriting company, states that "book projects on the shorter side, tailored to new markets like the Kindle Singles imprint and others (30,000–42,000 words) start at a cost of $15,000".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-washingtonpost.com_9-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-washingtonpost.com-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       And this shorter book appears to be here to stay. It was once financially impractical for publishers to produce such novella-length texts (they would have to charge too much); but this new market is, by 2015, already substantial and has been projected to be a solid part of the future of book publishing.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-theguardian.com_7-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-theguardian.com-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       So, with its appearance the starting price for the professional book writer has come down by about half, but only if this shorter format makes sense for the client.
      </p>
      <p>
       On the upper end of the spectrum, with celebrities that can all but guarantee a publisher large sales, the fees can be much higher. In 2001, the
       <i>
        New York Times
       </i>
       stated that the fee that the ghostwriter for
       <a href="/wiki/Hillary_Clinton" title="Hillary Clinton">
        Hillary Clinton
       </a>
       's memoirs will receive is probably about $500,000 of her book's $8 million advance, which "is near the top of flat fees paid to collaborators".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       There is also the consideration of different benchmarks in different countries. In Canada, The Writers' Union has established a minimum fee schedule for ghostwriting, starting at $40,000 for a 200–300 page book, paid at various stages of the drafting of the book. Research fees are an extra charge on top of this minimum fee.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In Germany, the average fee for a confidential ghostwriting service is about $100 per page.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2012)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       The Editorial Freelancers Association also suggests rates of 26 cents to 50 cents per word, which would be about $15,000 to $30,000 for a 250-page book.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       A recent availability also exists, of outsourcing many kinds of jobs, including ghostwriting, to
       <a href="/wiki/Offshoring" title="Offshoring">
        offshore
       </a>
       locations like India, China and the Philippines where the customer can save money.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
        <a href="#cite_note-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Outsource" title="Outsource">
        Outsourced
       </a>
       ghostwriters, whose quality levels vary widely, complete 200-page books for fees ranging between $3000 and $5000, or $12–$18 per page.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2012)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       The true tests of credibility—the writer's track record, and samples of his or her craft—become even more important in these instances, when the writer comes from a culture and first-language that are entirely different from the client's.
      </p>
      <p>
       In some cases, ghostwriters are allowed to share credit. For example, a common method is to put the client/author's name on a book cover as the main byline (by
       <i>
        Author's Name
       </i>
       ) and then to put the ghostwriter's name underneath it (as told to
       <i>
        Ghostwriter's Name
       </i>
       ). Sometimes this is done in lieu of pay or in order to decrease the amount of payment to the book ghostwriter for whom the credit has its own intrinsic value. Also, the ghostwriter can be cited as a coauthor of a book, or listed in the movie or film credits when having ghostwritten the script or screenplay for a film production.
      </p>
      <p>
       For nonfiction books, the ghostwriter may be credited as a "contributor" or a "research assistant". In other cases, the ghostwriter receives no official credit for writing a book or article; in cases where the credited author or the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Publisher" title="Publisher">
        publisher
       </a>
       or both wish to conceal the ghostwriter's role, the ghostwriter may be asked to sign a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nondisclosure_contract" title="Nondisclosure contract">
        nondisclosure contract
       </a>
       that legally forbids any mention of the writer's role in a project. Some have made the distinction between 'author' and 'writer,' as ghostwriter Kevin Anderson explains in a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington_Post" title="Washington Post">
        Washington Post
       </a>
       interview: "A ghostwriter is an interpreter and a translator, not an author, which is why our clients deserve full credit for authoring their books."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-washingtonpost.com_9-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-washingtonpost.com-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Types">
        Types
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghostwriter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Types">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Nonfiction">
        Nonfiction
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghostwriter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Nonfiction">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Ghostwriters are widely used by celebrities and public figures who wish to publish their autobiographies or memoirs. The degree of involvement of the ghostwriter in nonfiction writing projects ranges from minor to substantial. Various sources explain the role of the ghostwriter and how competent writers can get this kind of work. In some cases, a ghostwriter may be called in just to clean up, edit, and polish a rough draft of an autobiography or a "how-to" book. In other cases, the ghostwriter will write an entire book or article based on information, stories, notes, and an outline, interview sessions with the celebrity or public figure. The credited author also indicates to the ghostwriter what type of style, tone, or "voice" they want in the book.
      </p>
      <p>
       In some cases, such as with some "how-to" books, diet guides, or cookbooks, a book will be entirely written by a ghostwriter, and the celebrity (e.g., a well-known musician or sports star) will be credited as author. Publishing companies use this strategy to increase the marketability of a book by associating it with a celebrity or well-known figure. In several countries before elections, candidates commission ghostwriters to produce autobiographies for them so as to gain visibility and exposure. Two of
       <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">
        John F. Kennedy
       </a>
       's books are almost entirely credited to ghostwriters.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
        <a href="#cite_note-15">
         [15]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a href="/wiki/Donald_Trump" title="Donald Trump">
        Donald Trump
       </a>
       's famous
       <a href="/wiki/Trump:_The_Art_of_the_Deal" title="Trump: The Art of the Deal">
        autobiography
       </a>
       was produced by a ghostwriter.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-trump_16-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-trump-16">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Several of Hillary Clinton's books were also produced by ghost writers.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
        <a href="#cite_note-17">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       <br/>
       A consultant or career-switcher may pay to have a book ghostwritten on a topic in their professional area, to establish or enhance their credibility as an 'expert' in their field. For example, a successful salesperson hoping to become a motivational speaker on selling may pay a ghostwriter to write a book on sales techniques. Often this type of book is published by a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Self-publishing_press" title="Self-publishing press">
        self-publishing press
       </a>
       (or "
       <a href="/wiki/Vanity_press" title="Vanity press">
        vanity press
       </a>
       "), which means that the author is paying to have the book published. This type of book is typically given away to prospective clients as a promotional tool, rather than being sold in bookstores.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Fiction">
        Fiction
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghostwriter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Fiction">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Ghostwriters are employed by fiction publishers for several reasons. In some cases, publishers use ghostwriters to increase the number of books that can be published each year by a well-known, highly marketable author. Ghostwriters are mostly used to pen fiction works for well-known, "name" authors in genres such as detective fiction, mysteries, and teen fiction.
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:The_Tower_Treasure_(Hardy_Boys_no._1,_revised_edition_-_front_cover).jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="393" data-file-width="252" height="265" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/0/05/The_Tower_Treasure_%28Hardy_Boys_no._1%2C_revised_edition_-_front_cover%29.jpg/170px-The_Tower_Treasure_%28Hardy_Boys_no._1%2C_revised_edition_-_front_cover%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/0/05/The_Tower_Treasure_%28Hardy_Boys_no._1%2C_revised_edition_-_front_cover%29.jpg 1.5x" width="170"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:The_Tower_Treasure_(Hardy_Boys_no._1,_revised_edition_-_front_cover).jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Cover of the revised edition of
         <i>
          <a href="/wiki/The_Tower_Treasure" title="The Tower Treasure">
           The Tower Treasure
          </a>
         </i>
         , the first Hardy Boys mystery.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Additionally, publishers use ghostwriters to write new books for established series where the 'author' is a
       <a href="/wiki/Pseudonym" title="Pseudonym">
        pseudonym
       </a>
       . For example, the purported authors of the
       <a href="/wiki/Nancy_Drew" title="Nancy Drew">
        Nancy Drew
       </a>
       and
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hardy_Boys" title="Hardy Boys">
        Hardy Boys
       </a>
       mysteries, "
       <a href="/wiki/Carolyn_Keene" title="Carolyn Keene">
        Carolyn Keene
       </a>
       " and "
       <a href="/wiki/Franklin_W._Dixon" title="Franklin W. Dixon">
        Franklin W. Dixon
       </a>
       ", respectively, are actually pseudonyms for a series of ghostwriters who write books in the same style using a template of basic information about the book's characters and their fictional universe (names, dates, speech patterns), and about the tone and style that are expected in the book (for more information, see the articles on
       <a href="/wiki/Pseudonym" title="Pseudonym">
        pseudonyms
       </a>
       or
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pen_names" title="Pen names">
        pen names
       </a>
       ). In addition, ghostwriters are often given copies of several of the previous books in the series to help them match the style.
      </p>
      <p>
       The well-known web publicist Keith Acton rose to underground notoriety and disdain when it was discovered he had paid a ghostwriter to write most of his work. Moreover, the estate of romance novelist
       <a href="/wiki/V._C._Andrews" title="V. C. Andrews">
        V. C. Andrews
       </a>
       hired ghostwriter
       <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Neiderman" title="Andrew Neiderman">
        Andrew Neiderman
       </a>
       to continue writing novels after her death, under her name and in a similar style to her original works. Many of action writer
       <a href="/wiki/Tom_Clancy" title="Tom Clancy">
        Tom Clancy
       </a>
       's books from the
       <a href="/wiki/2000s_(decade)" title="2000s (decade)">
        2000s
       </a>
       bear the names of two people on their covers, with Clancy's name in larger print and the other author's name in smaller print. Various books bearing Clancy's name were written by different authors under the same pseudonym. The first two books in the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Tom_Clancy%27s_Splinter_Cell" title="Tom Clancy's Splinter Cell">
         Tom Clancy's Splinter Cell
        </a>
       </i>
       franchise were written by
       <a href="/wiki/Raymond_Benson" title="Raymond Benson">
        Raymond Benson
       </a>
       under the pseudonym
       <a href="/wiki/David_Michaels_(author)" title="David Michaels (author)">
        David Michaels
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       Sometimes famous authors will ghostwrite for other celebrities as well, such as when
       <a href="/wiki/H._P._Lovecraft" title="H. P. Lovecraft">
        H. P. Lovecraft
       </a>
       ghostwrote "Imprisoned with the Pharaohs" (also known as "Under the Pyramids") for
       <a href="/wiki/Harry_Houdini" title="Harry Houdini">
        Harry Houdini
       </a>
       in
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Weird_Tales" title="Weird Tales">
         Weird Tales
        </a>
       </i>
       in the 1920s.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2010)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Religious">
        Religious
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghostwriter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Religious">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       A number of papal
       <a href="/wiki/Encyclical" title="Encyclical">
        encyclicals
       </a>
       have been written by ghostwriters.
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pascendi" title="Pascendi">
         Pascendi
        </a>
       </i>
       , for instance, was written by Joseph Lemius (1860–1923), the procurator in Rome of the Oblates of Mary Immaculate.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">
        <a href="#cite_note-18">
         [18]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In June 1938,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pius_XI" title="Pius XI">
        Pius XI
       </a>
       summoned
       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">
        American
       </a>
       <a href="/wiki/Society_of_Jesus" title="Society of Jesus">
        Jesuit
       </a>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_LaFarge,_Jr." title="John LaFarge, Jr.">
        John La Farge
       </a>
       , who began to prepare a draft of
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Humani_generis_unitas" title="Humani generis unitas">
         Humani generis unitas
        </a>
       </i>
       , which LaFarge and two other Jesuits—Gustav Gundlach and Gustave Desbuquois
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bailey_19-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-bailey-19">
         [19]
        </a>
       </sup>
       —on in Paris; the draft was approximately 100 pages long.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-chronicle_20-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-chronicle-20">
         [20]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Another Jesuit translated the draft encyclical into Latin, presenting it to
       <a href="/wiki/Wlodimir_Led%C3%B3chowski" title="Wlodimir Ledóchowski">
        Wlodimir Ledóchowski
       </a>
       , then the General of the Society of Jesus who had chosen Gundlach and Desbuquois for the project.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bailey_19-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-bailey-19">
         [19]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The draft encyclical was delivered to the Vatican in September 1938.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bailey_19-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-bailey-19">
         [19]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sebastian_Tromp" title="Sebastian Tromp">
        Sebastian Tromp
       </a>
       , a Dutch Jesuit, a solid
       <a href="/wiki/Thomism" title="Thomism">
        Thomist
       </a>
       theologian and close to Pope
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pius_XII" title="Pius XII">
        Pius XII
       </a>
       , is considered to be the main ghostwriter of
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mystici_corporis" title="Mystici corporis">
         Mystici corporis
        </a>
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
        <a href="#cite_note-21">
         [21]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Academic">
        Academic
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghostwriter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Academic">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       There are ghostwriting companies
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
        <a href="#cite_note-22">
         [22]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
        <a href="#cite_note-23">
         [23]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and freelancers
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
        <a href="#cite_note-24">
         [24]
        </a>
       </sup>
       that sell entrance essays, term papers, theses and dissertations to students. Such services are sometimes offered by what is referred to as
       <a href="/wiki/Essay_mill" title="Essay mill">
        essay mills
       </a>
       and frequently transacted through online interfaces.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
        <a href="#cite_note-25">
         [25]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Despite being considered unethical and leading to repercussions if detected by universities,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph.co.uk_26-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-telegraph.co.uk-26">
         [26]
        </a>
       </sup>
       academic ghostwriting does not represent illegal activity in the United States and United Kingdom.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">
        <a href="#cite_note-27">
         [27]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Although academic ghostwriting involves the sale of academic texts that are written on demand, it cannot be equated with
       <a href="/wiki/Plagiarism" title="Plagiarism">
        plagiarism
       </a>
       , since it does not involve an undisclosed appropriation of existing texts. As opposed to cases of
       <a href="/wiki/Plagiarism" title="Plagiarism">
        plagiarism
       </a>
       that stem from a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Copy-and-paste" title="Copy-and-paste">
        copy-and-paste
       </a>
       reuse of previous work, essays and assignments that are obtained through ghostwriting services as a rule have the originality of their text confirmed by
       <a href="/wiki/Plagiarism_detection" title="Plagiarism detection">
        plagiarism detection
       </a>
       software packages or online services that are widely used by universities.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraph.co.uk_26-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-telegraph.co.uk-26">
         [26]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Universities have developed strategies to combat this type of academic services, which can be associated with
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academic_fraud" title="Academic fraud">
        academic fraud
       </a>
       , that are offered to students and researchers. Some universities allow professors to give students
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oral_examination" title="Oral examination">
        oral examinations
       </a>
       on papers which a professor believes to be 'ghostwritten.’ If the student is unfamiliar with the content of an essay that he or she has submitted, then the student can be charged with
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Academic_fraud" title="Academic fraud">
        academic fraud
       </a>
       . However, academic ghostwriting
       <i>
        per se
       </i>
       does not lead to
       <a href="/wiki/Plagiarism" title="Plagiarism">
        plagiarism
       </a>
       , as is demonstrated by the widely accepted and applied practice of
       <a href="/wiki/Legal_ghostwriting" title="Legal ghostwriting">
        legal ghostwriting
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">
        <a href="#cite_note-28">
         [28]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">
        <a href="#cite_note-29">
         [29]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">
        <a href="#cite_note-30">
         [30]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Medical">
        Medical
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghostwriter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Medical">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Medical_ghostwriter" title="Medical ghostwriter">
        Medical ghostwriter
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       With medical ghostwriting, pharmaceutical companies pay professional writers to produce papers and then pay other scientists or physicians to attach their names to these papers before they are published in medical or scientific journals. Medical ghostwriting has been criticized by a variety of professional organizations
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wame_31-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-wame-31">
         [31]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32">
        <a href="#cite_note-32">
         [32]
        </a>
       </sup>
       representing the drug industry, publishers, and medical societies, and it may violate American laws prohibiting off-label promotion by drug manufacturers as well as anti-kickback provisions within the statutes governing Medicare.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33">
        <a href="#cite_note-33">
         [33]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Recently, it has attracted scrutiny from the lay press
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">
        <a href="#cite_note-34">
         [34]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and from lawmakers,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35">
        <a href="#cite_note-35">
         [35]
        </a>
       </sup>
       as well. It is permitted at some institutions, including the
       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Washington_School_of_Medicine" title="University of Washington School of Medicine">
        University of Washington School of Medicine
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36">
        <a href="#cite_note-36">
         [36]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37">
        <a href="#cite_note-37">
         [37]
        </a>
       </sup>
       while it is prohibited and considered a particularly pernicious form of
       <a href="/wiki/Plagiarism" title="Plagiarism">
        plagiarism
       </a>
       at others, such as
       <a href="/wiki/Tufts_University_School_of_Medicine" title="Tufts University School of Medicine">
        Tufts University School of Medicine
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38">
        <a href="#cite_note-38">
         [38]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Medical_writing" title="Medical writing">
        Professional medical writers
       </a>
       can write papers without being listed as authors of the paper and without being considered ghostwriters, provided their role is acknowledged. The
       <a href="/wiki/European_Medical_Writers_Association" title="European Medical Writers Association">
        European Medical Writers Association
       </a>
       have published guidelines which aim to ensure professional medical writers carry out this role in an ethical and responsible manner.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39">
        <a href="#cite_note-39">
         [39]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The use of properly acknowledged medical writers is accepted as legitimate by organisations such as the World Association of Medical Editors
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wame_31-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-wame-31">
         [31]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and the British Medical Journal.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40">
        <a href="#cite_note-40">
         [40]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Moreover, professional medical writers' expertise in presenting scientific data may be of benefit in producing better quality papers.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41">
        <a href="#cite_note-41">
         [41]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">

         </a>

          </b>

            adding to it

      <p>
       Some websites, including
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Blogs" title="Blogs">
        blogs
       </a>
       , are ghostwritten, because not all authors have the
       <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">
        information technology
       </a>
       skills or the time to dedicate to running a website. Nonetheless, the style, tone and content is modeled on that of the credited author. Many website ghostwriters are
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freelance" title="Freelance">
        freelance
       </a>
       but some are
       <a href="/wiki/Freelancer" title="Freelancer">
        freelancers
       </a>
       who work under
       <a href="/wiki/Employment_contract" title="Employment contract">
        contract
       </a>
       , as with
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radio_presenter" title="Radio presenter">
        radio presenters
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Television_presenter" title="Television presenter">
        television presenters
       </a>
       . Occasionally
       <a href="/wiki/Pseudonym#Literary_pen_names" title="Pseudonym">
        a "house pseudonym", or collective name
       </a>
       is used by the author of the website.
      </p>
      <p>
       Some celebrities,
       <a href="/wiki/Chief_executive_officer" title="Chief executive officer">
        CEOs
       </a>
       , or public figures set up blog websites—sometimes as a marketing, public relations, or lobbying tool. However, since these individuals are typically too busy to write their blog posts, they hire discreet ghostwriters to post to the blog under the celebrity or CEO's name. As with nonfiction ghostwriting, the blog ghostwriter models their writing style, content and tone on that of the credited author.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Music">
        Music
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghostwriter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Music">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <h4>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Classical_music_and_film_scores">
        Classical music and film scores
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghostwriter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Classical music and film scores">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h4>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Martini_bologna_mozart_1777.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3460" data-file-width="3003" height="196" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Martini_bologna_mozart_1777.jpg/170px-Martini_bologna_mozart_1777.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Martini_bologna_mozart_1777.jpg/255px-Martini_bologna_mozart_1777.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Martini_bologna_mozart_1777.jpg/340px-Martini_bologna_mozart_1777.jpg 2x" width="170"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Martini_bologna_mozart_1777.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         The Classical era composer
         <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_Amadeus_Mozart" title="Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart">
          Mozart
         </a>
         was paid to ghostwrite music for wealthy patrons who wished to give the impression that they were gifted composers.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Wolfgang_Amadeus_Mozart" title="Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart">
        Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart
       </a>
       is an example of a well-known composer who was paid to ghostwrite music for wealthy patrons. More recently, composers such as the UK-based Patric Standford (born in 1939) have ghostwritten for symphonic recordings and films such as the Rod McKuen Cello Concerto.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42">
        <a href="#cite_note-42">
         [42]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In the film industry, a music ghostwriter is a "person who composes music for another composer but is not credited on the cue sheet or in the final product in any way." The practice is considered one of the "dirty little secrets of the film and television music business" that is considered unethical,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43">
        <a href="#cite_note-43">
         [43]
        </a>
       </sup>
       but has been common since the early stages of the film industry. In the early years of film,
       <a href="/wiki/David_Raksin" title="David Raksin">
        David Raksin
       </a>
       worked as music ghostwriter and
       <a href="/wiki/Orchestration" title="Orchestration">
        orchestrator
       </a>
       for
       <a href="/wiki/Charlie_Chaplin" title="Charlie Chaplin">
        Charlie Chaplin
       </a>
       ; even though Chaplin was credited as the score writer, he was considered to be a "hummer" (pejorative film industry slang for a person who purports to be a film score composer but who in fact only gives a general idea of the melodies to a ghostwriter).
      </p>
      <p>
       The practice is also common in television, as composers listed on cue sheets are entitled to music royalties every time an episode or theme score appears on television. A 1998 investigation by
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">
         The Hollywood Reporter
        </a>
       </i>
       revealed that it was especially prevalent among animation companies such as
       <a href="/wiki/Saban_Entertainment" title="Saban Entertainment">
        Saban Entertainment
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/DiC_Entertainment" title="DiC Entertainment">
        DiC
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ruby-Spears_Productions" title="Ruby-Spears Productions">
        Ruby-Spears Productions
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Hanna-Barbera" title="Hanna-Barbera">
        Hanna-Barbera
       </a>
       , which often listed company executives as musicians for the purpose of royalties.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44">
        <a href="#cite_note-44">
         [44]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Several composers later filed a multimillion-dollar lawsuit against Saban Entertainment president
       <a href="/wiki/Haim_Saban" title="Haim Saban">
        Haim Saban
       </a>
       , for allegedly taking ownership and credit for their musical compositions.
      </p>
      <h4>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Popular_music">
        Popular music
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghostwriter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Popular music">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h4>
      <p>
       Musical ghostwriting also occurs in
       <a href="/wiki/Popular_music" title="Popular music">
        popular music
       </a>
       . When a record company wants to market an inexperienced young singer as a
       <a href="/wiki/Singer-songwriter" title="Singer-songwriter">
        singer-songwriter
       </a>
       , or help a veteran bandleader coping with writer's block (or a lack of motivation to finish the next album), an experienced songwriter may be discreetly brought in to help.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2013)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       In other cases, a ghostwriter writes lyrics and a melody in the style of the credited musician, with little or no input from the credited musician. A ghostwriter providing this type of service may be thanked, without reference to the service provided, in the album credits, or they may be a true 'ghost', with no acknowledgement in the album.
      </p>
      <p>
       Legal disputes have arisen when musical ghostwriters have tried to claim royalties, when an allegedly ghostwritten song becomes a money-making hit. In 1987, Darryl Neudorf was asked to work on a project for
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nettwerk_Productions" title="Nettwerk Productions">
        Nettwerk Productions
       </a>
       involving a newly signed artist in their repertoire named
       <a href="/wiki/Sarah_McLachlan" title="Sarah McLachlan">
        Sarah McLachlan
       </a>
       . This recording, the album
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Touch_(Sarah_McLachlan_album)" title="Touch (Sarah McLachlan album)">
         Touch
        </a>
       </i>
       , resulted in garnering the interest of
       <a href="/wiki/Arista_Records" title="Arista Records">
        Arista Records
       </a>
       . She signed a multi-album contract with them and two of the songs that Neudorf worked on with her became commercial hits in
       <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">
        Canada
       </a>
       . In 1991, Neudorf was invited back to work with McLachlan on her second album,
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Solace_(Sarah_McLachlan_album)" title="Solace (Sarah McLachlan album)">
         Solace
        </a>
       </i>
       . In 1993, he filed a lawsuit against McLachlan and her label,
       <a href="/wiki/Nettwerk" title="Nettwerk">
        Nettwerk
       </a>
       , alleging that he had made a significant and uncredited contribution to the songwriting on
       <i>
        Touch
       </i>
       , and alleging that he wasn't paid properly for work done on
       <i>
        Solace
       </i>
       . The judge in this suit eventually ruled in McLachlan's favor on the songs; though Neudorf may have contributed to the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Songwriting" title="Songwriting">
        songwriting
       </a>
       , neither regarded each other as joint authors. The judge ruled in Neudorf's favour on the payment issue.
      </p>
      <div class="thumb tleft">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Chuck_D._Slakthuset_i_Malm%C3%B6_1991.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="659" data-file-width="1234" height="134" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Chuck_D._Slakthuset_i_Malm%C3%B6_1991.jpg/250px-Chuck_D._Slakthuset_i_Malm%C3%B6_1991.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Chuck_D._Slakthuset_i_Malm%C3%B6_1991.jpg/375px-Chuck_D._Slakthuset_i_Malm%C3%B6_1991.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/00/Chuck_D._Slakthuset_i_Malm%C3%B6_1991.jpg/500px-Chuck_D._Slakthuset_i_Malm%C3%B6_1991.jpg 2x" width="250"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Chuck_D._Slakthuset_i_Malm%C3%B6_1991.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Chuck D of
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_Enemy_(group)" title="Public Enemy (group)">
          Public Enemy
         </a>
         has offered a more positive view of ghostwriting in hip hop.
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       In
       <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">
        hip hop music
       </a>
       , the increasing use of ghostwriters by high-profile hip hop stars has led to controversy. Critics view the increasing use of hip hop ghostwriters as the "perversion of hip-hop by commerce." This is because of the limiting definition of "rapping" as "...about you expressing yourself through your own words, not someone else’s."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45">
        <a href="#cite_note-45">
         [45]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Chuck D of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_Enemy_(group)" title="Public Enemy (group)">
        Public Enemy
       </a>
       thinks this point of view is mistaken because "...not everyone is equipped to be a lyricist and not everyone is equipped to be a vocalist."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46">
        <a href="#cite_note-46">
         [46]
        </a>
       </sup>
       He points out that creating a rap song may require multiple talents.
       <a href="/wiki/Frank_Ocean" title="Frank Ocean">
        Frank Ocean
       </a>
       started his career as a ghostwriter for artists such as
       <a href="/wiki/Justin_Bieber" title="Justin Bieber">
        Justin Bieber
       </a>
       , Damienn Jones,
       <a href="/wiki/John_Legend" title="John Legend">
        John Legend
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Brandy_Norwood" title="Brandy Norwood">
        Brandy
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <table class="cquote pullquote" role="presentation" style="margin:auto; border-collapse: collapse; border: none; background-color: transparent; width: auto;">
       <tr>
        <td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: top; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: left; padding: 10px 10px;">
         “
        </td>
        <td style="vertical-align: top; border: none; padding: 4px 10px;">
         The practice of ghostwriting is one of rap's biggest taboos, and yet many of its greatest hits were ghostwritten. So who are Hip Hop's ghostwriters and what place do they have in a style of music built on speaking from the heart? Not all singers are songwriters
         <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc.com_1-1">
          <a href="#cite_note-bbc.com-1">
           [1]
          </a>
         </sup>
        </td>
        <td style="width: 20px; vertical-align: bottom; border: none; color: #B2B7F2; font-size: 40px; font-family: 'Times New Roman', Times, serif; font-weight: bold; line-height: .6em; text-align: right; padding: 10px 10px;">
         ”
        </td>
       </tr>
      </table>
      <p>
       Currently in hip-hop, the credit given to ghostwriters varies: "silent pens might sign confidentiality clauses, appear obliquely in the liner notes, or discuss their participation freely." In some cases, liner notes credit individuals for "vocal arrangement", which may be a euphemism for ghostwriting. In the late 2000s (decade), hip-hop ghostwriting services like
       <a href="/wiki/Rap_Rebirth" title="Rap Rebirth">
        Rap Rebirth
       </a>
       ,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47">
        <a href="#cite_note-47">
         [47]
        </a>
       </sup>
       have appeared online, which provide recording artists who wish to purchase ghostwritten rhymes a greater degree of anonymity.
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Visual_art">
        Visual art
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghostwriter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Visual art">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Ghost-authorship also applies to the visual arts, most commonly paintings. The extent of the master artist's contribution varies widely, as little as composition adjustments and corrective brush strokes, or as much as entire works. A common practice is use of the art instruction class milieu in which the master artist makes significant contributions to the work of the student who then signs that work as his or her own. Services addressing complete works have historically been highly confidential. Less prevalent are advertised commercial services which may use the term "vanity artwork" as suggestive of "vanity publishing".
      </p>
      <div style="clear:both;">
      </div>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="As_blacklisting_countermeasure">
        As blacklisting countermeasure
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ghostwriter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: As blacklisting countermeasure">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       In countries where the freedom of speech is not upheld and authors that have somehow displeased the ruling regime are "blacklisted" (i.e. forbidden from having their works published), the blacklisted authors or composers may ghostwrite material for other authors or composers who are in the good graces of the regime.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2010)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       Some blacklisted communist sympathisers have won Academy Awards.
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Carl_Foreman" title="Carl Foreman">
         Carl Foreman
        </a>
        and
        <a href="/wiki/Michael_Wilson_(writer)" title="Michael Wilson (writer)">
         Michael Wilson
        </a>
        for
        <i>
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bridge_on_the_River_Kwai" title="Bridge on the River Kwai">
          Bridge on the River Kwai
         </a>
        </i>
        (credited to
        <a href="/wiki/Pierre_Boulle" title="Pierre Boulle">
         Pierre Boulle
        </a>
        , who wrote
        <a href="/wiki/The_Bridge_over_the_River_Kwai" title="The Bridge over the River Kwai">
         the novel
        </a>
        ).
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Dalton_Trumbo" title="Dalton Trumbo">
         Dalton Trumbo
        </a>
        for
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Roman_Holiday" title="Roman Holiday">
          Roman Holiday
         </a>
        </i>
        (credited to
        <a href="/wiki/Ian_McLellan_Hunter" title="Ian McLellan Hunter">
         Ian McLellan Hunter
        </a>
        ).
       </li>
      </ul>
