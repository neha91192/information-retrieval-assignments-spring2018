       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US" title="US">

        key grip
       </b>
       supervises all
       <a href="/wiki/Grip_(job)" title="Grip (job)">
        grip
       </a>
       (lighting and rigging) crews and reports to the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Director_of_photography" title="Director of photography">
        director of photography
       </a>
       .

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Responsibilities">
        Responsibilities
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Key_grip&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Responsibilities">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Before filming begins, the key grip attends location scouts and meets the director of photography to determine what additional tools (location-specific motor vehicles, dollies, cranes, mounts, etc.) will be needed, orders and prepares required equipment, and transports equipment to the filming location.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Uva_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Uva-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Once on set, the key grip coordinates with the electric and camera departments alongside the director of photography to control lighting and camera movement and setup.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dzyaz_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Dzyaz-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       As a supervisor, the key grip directs the crew of grips, many with specialized skills such as dolly grips, crane operators, or special equipment operators.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Uva_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Uva-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The key grip is sometimes credited as the "first company grip."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Safety">
        Safety
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Key_grip&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Safety">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The key grip is frequently asked to be the safety monitor of the film set. Changes in the film and television worlds have created lasting change in crew responsibilities. Any union production crews working under the "Hollywood" umbrella are required to take
       <a href="/wiki/Occupational_Safety_and_Health_Administration" title="Occupational Safety and Health Administration">
        Occupational Safety and Health Administration
       </a>
       -style certified courses in safety. The first AD (first
       <a href="/wiki/Assistant_director" title="Assistant director">
        assistant director
       </a>
       ) is responsible for the safe operation of the set. The key grip no longer has that distinction in newer Hollywood policy.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="In_other_countries">
        In other countries
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Key_grip&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In other countries">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In
       <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain" title="Great Britain">
        Great Britain
       </a>
       the "grips" are part of the camera department exclusively and are not generally responsible for regular grip duties as they are in the United States.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Skills_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Skills-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In
       <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">
        Australia
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">
        New Zealand
       </a>
       the key grip generally owns the grip equipment, often consisting of dollies, track, cranes, camera cars, and insert trailers.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2012)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
