      <p>
       <i>
        <b>
         Story of a Bad Woman
        </b>
       </i>
       (Spanish:
       <i>
        <b>
         Historia de una mala mujer
        </b>
       </i>
       ) is a 1948 Argentine
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Drama_film" title="Drama film">
        drama film
       </a>
       directed by
       <a href="/wiki/Luis_Saslavsky" title="Luis Saslavsky">
        Luis Saslavsky
       </a>
       and starring the Mexican and Hollywood superstar
       <a href="/wiki/Dolores_del_R%C3%ADo" title="Dolores del Río">
        Dolores del Río
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mar%C3%ADa_Duval" title="María Duval">
        María Duval
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Alberto_Closas" title="Alberto Closas">
        Alberto Closas
       </a>
       . It is based on
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Lady_Windermere%27s_Fan" title="Lady Windermere's Fan">
         Lady Windermere's Fan
        </a>
       </i>
       by
       <a href="/wiki/Oscar_Wilde" title="Oscar Wilde">
        Oscar Wilde
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The film's
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Art_direction" title="Art direction">
        art direction
       </a>
       was by
       <a href="/wiki/Ra%C3%BAl_Soldi" title="Raúl Soldi">
        Raúl Soldi
       </a>
       . It was made by
       <a href="/wiki/Argentina_Sono_Film" title="Argentina Sono Film">
        Argentina Sono Film
       </a>
       , one of the country's biggest studios during the era.
      </p>
