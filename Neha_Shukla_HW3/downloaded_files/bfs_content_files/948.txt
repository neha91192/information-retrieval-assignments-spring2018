      <p>
       The
       <b>
        Writers Guild of America
       </b>
       is the joint efforts of two different US labor unions representing TV and film writers:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        The
        <a href="/wiki/Writers_Guild_of_America,_East" title="Writers Guild of America, East">
         Writers Guild of America, East
        </a>
        (WGAE), headquartered in
        <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">
         New York City
        </a>
        .
       </li>
       <li>
        The
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Writers_Guild_of_America,_West" title="Writers Guild of America, West">
         Writers Guild of America, West
        </a>
        (WGAW), headquartered in
        <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">
         Los Angeles
        </a>
        .
       </li>
      </ul>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Common_activities">
        Common activities
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Writers_Guild_of_America&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Common activities">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       The WGAE and WGAW negotiate contracts in unison as well as launch
       <a href="/wiki/Strike_action" title="Strike action">
        strike actions
       </a>
       simultaneously:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_Writers_Guild_of_America_strike" title="2007–08 Writers Guild of America strike">
         2007–08 Writers Guild of America strike
        </a>
        <ul>
         <li>
          <a href="/wiki/Effect_of_the_2007%E2%80%9308_Writers_Guild_of_America_strike_on_television" title="Effect of the 2007–08 Writers Guild of America strike on television">
           Effect of the 2007–08 Writers Guild of America strike on television
          </a>
          , a list of television shows affected by the strike
         </li>
        </ul>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/1988_Writers_Guild_of_America_strike" title="1988 Writers Guild of America strike">
         1988 Writers Guild of America strike
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        1985 Writers Guild of America strike
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/1981_Writers_Guild_of_America_strike" title="1981 Writers Guild of America strike">
         1981 Writers Guild of America strike
        </a>
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/1960_Writers_Guild_of_America_strike" title="1960 Writers Guild of America strike">
         1960 Writers Guild of America strike
        </a>
       </li>
      </ul>
      <p>
       Although each Guild runs independently, they perform some activities in parallel:
      </p>
      <ul>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/Writers_Guild_of_America_Award" title="Writers Guild of America Award">
         Writers Guild of America Award
        </a>
        , an annual awards show with simultaneous presentations on each coast
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/WGA_screenwriting_credit_system" title="WGA screenwriting credit system">
         WGA screenwriting credit system
        </a>
        , determines how writers' names are listed during the credits
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/WGA_script_registration_service" title="WGA script registration service">
         WGA script registration service
        </a>
        , online services to prove when scripts were written and by whom
       </li>
       <li>
        <a href="/wiki/International_Affiliation_of_Writers_Guilds" title="International Affiliation of Writers Guilds">
         International Affiliation of Writers Guilds
        </a>
        (IAWG), both Guilds belong to this international labor federation
       </li>
      </ul>
