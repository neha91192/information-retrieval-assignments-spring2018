     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       A
       <b>
        television program creator
       </b>
       is typically the person who pitches a new
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_program" title="Television program">
        TV show
       </a>
       idea and sees it through. There are numerous notable
       <b>
        television program creators
       </b>
       ; this article includes a list of many of them.
      </p>
      <p>
       Often, the creator is the
       <a href="/wiki/Screenwriter" title="Screenwriter">
        writer
       </a>
       of the
       <a href="/wiki/Pilot_(television_episode)" title="Pilot (television episode)">
        pilot episode
       </a>
       , or a
       <a href="/wiki/Television_director" title="Television director">
        director
       </a>
       , or a
       <a href="/wiki/Television_producer" title="Television producer">
        producer
       </a>
       . Sometimes it is a writer of the series' "bible", or writers' guidelines.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In the United States, a
       <a href="/wiki/WGA_screenwriting_credit_system" title="WGA screenwriting credit system">
        Writers Guild of America (WGA) screenwriting credit system
       </a>
       governs credits. For example, the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Writers_Guild_of_America,_West" title="Writers Guild of America, West">
        Writers Guild of America, West
       </a>
       provides specifications for creator credits that govern its members.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wgacc_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-wgacc-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Producers_Guild_of_America" title="Producers Guild of America">
        Producers Guild of America
       </a>
       's corresponding code for producers defines "Executive Producer" and similar roles but not an explicit "Creator" role.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pg_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-pg-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pg2_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-pg2-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">
          <span title='"You'>
           not in citation given
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Creator is a specific credit given explicitly in many shows. However, it has not always been a prominent, explicit credit. For example,
       <a href="/wiki/Sydney_Newman" title="Sydney Newman">
        Sydney Newman
       </a>
       , the accepted creator of
       <a href="/wiki/The_Avengers_(TV_series)" title="The Avengers (TV series)">
        <i>
         The Avengers
        </i>
       </a>
       (1961–69), was never given an explicit credit as creator; Newman never thought to ask for one.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The creator of a television show may retain rights to participate in profits, often to be paid by the production company as a percentage of fees that it receives from networks and distributors.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-vault_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-vault-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In 2014, for prime-time network TV shows, the WGA-required royalty to be paid to a writer with "created by" credit is approximately $1,000 per episode or higher.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-writers_7-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-writers-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Who merits creator credit is sometimes a matter of contention. In a 2013 legal case, a director sued a former writing partner for co-creator credit.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
