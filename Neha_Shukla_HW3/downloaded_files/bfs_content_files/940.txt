      <p>
       A
       <b>
        film director
       </b>
       is a person who directs the making of a
       <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">
        film
       </a>
       . A film director controls a film's artistic and dramatic aspects and visualizes the
       <a href="/wiki/Screenplay" title="Screenplay">
        screenplay
       </a>
       (or script) while guiding the technical crew and actors in the fulfillment of that
       <a href="/wiki/Goal" title="Goal">
        vision
       </a>
       . The director has a key role in choosing the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cast_member" title="Cast member">
        cast members
       </a>
       , production design, and the creative aspects of
       <a href="/wiki/Filmmaking" title="Filmmaking">
        filmmaking
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Under
       <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">
        European Union
       </a>
       law, the director is viewed as the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Auteur_theory" title="Auteur theory">
        author
       </a>
       of the film.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Google_Books_2-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Google_Books-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The film director gives direction to the cast and crew and creates an overall vision through which a film eventually becomes realized, or noticed. Directors need to be able to mediate differences in creative visions and stay within the boundaries of the film's
       <a href="/wiki/Budget" title="Budget">
        budget
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
       There are many pathways to becoming a film director. Some film directors started as screenwriters,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cinematographers" title="Cinematographers">
        cinematographers
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Film_editor" title="Film editor">
        film editors
       </a>
       or actors. Other film directors have attended a film school. Directors use different approaches. Some
       <a href="/wiki/Outline_(list)" title="Outline (list)">
        outline
       </a>
       a general plotline and let the actors improvise dialogue, while others control every aspect, and demand that the actors and crew follow instructions precisely. Some directors also write their own screenplays or collaborate on screenplays with long-standing writing partners. Some directors edit or appear in their films, or compose the music score for their films.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-career_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-career-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Responsibility">
        Responsibility
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Film_director&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Responsibility">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:LondonSmog.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1024" data-file-width="768" height="227" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/LondonSmog.jpg/170px-LondonSmog.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/LondonSmog.jpg/255px-LondonSmog.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/LondonSmog.jpg/340px-LondonSmog.jpg 2x" width="170"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:LondonSmog.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         The film director gives last minute direction to the cast and crew, while filming a
         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Costume_drama" title="Costume drama">
          costume drama
         </a>
         on location in
         <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">
          London
         </a>
         .
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Film directors create an overall vision through which a film eventually becomes realized/noticed.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Film_schools_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Film_schools-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Realizing this vision includes overseeing the artistic and technical elements of film production, as well as directing the shooting
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Timetable" title="Timetable">
        timetable
       </a>
       and meeting deadlines.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-career_3-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-career-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Media_College_5-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Media_College-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       This entails organizing the
       <a href="/wiki/Film_crew" title="Film crew">
        film crew
       </a>
       in such a way to achieve their vision of the film.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Studentpulse_6-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Studentpulse-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       This requires skills of group leadership, as well as the ability to maintain a singular focus even in the stressful, fast-paced environment of a film set.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Career_Centre_8-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Career_Centre-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Moreover, it is necessary to have an artistic eye to frame shots and to give precise feedback to cast and crew,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Diploma_Guide_9-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Diploma_Guide-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       thus, excellent communication skills are a must.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-National_Career_Service_10-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-National_Career_Service-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Since the film director depends on the successful cooperation of many different creative individuals with possibly strongly contradicting artistic ideals and visions, he or she also needs to possess conflict resolution skills in order to mediate whenever necessary.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Thus the director ensures that all individuals involved in the film production are working towards an identical vision for the completed film.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Studentpulse_6-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Studentpulse-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The set of varying challenges he or she has to tackle has been described as "a multi-dimensional jigsaw puzzle with egos and weather thrown in for good measure".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Directors_UK_12-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Directors_UK-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It adds to the pressure that the success of a film can influence when and how they will work again.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Omnipresent are the boundaries of the films budget.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
        <a href="#cite_note-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Additionally, the director may also have to ensure an intended
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Motion_picture_rating_system" title="Motion picture rating system">
        age rating
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
        <a href="#cite_note-15">
         [15]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Theoretically the sole superior of a director is the studio that is financing the film,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Film_schools_4-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Film_schools-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Education_Portal_16-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Education_Portal-16">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
       however a poor working relationship between a film director and an actor could possibly result in the director being replaced if the actor is a major
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Film_star" title="Film star">
        film star
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
        <a href="#cite_note-17">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Even so, it is arguable that the director spends more time on a project than anyone else, considering that the director is one of the few positions that requires intimate involvement during every stage of film production. Thus, the position of film director is widely considered to be a highly stressful and demanding one.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Princeton_18-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Princeton-18">
         [18]
        </a>
       </sup>
       It has been said that "20-hour days are not unusual".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Film_schools_4-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Film_schools-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Under
       <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">
        European Union
       </a>
       law, the film director is considered the "author" or one of the authors of a film, largely as a result of the influence of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Auteur_theory" title="Auteur theory">
        auteur theory
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Google_Books_2-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Google_Books-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Auteur theory is a
       <a href="/wiki/Film_criticism" title="Film criticism">
        film criticism
       </a>
       concept that holds that a film director's
       <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">
        film
       </a>
       reflects the director's personal creative vision, as if they were the primary "auteur" (the
       <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">
        French
       </a>
       word for "author").
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">
        <a href="#cite_note-19">
         [19]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In spite of—and sometimes even because of—the production of the film as part of an industrial process, the auteur's creative voice is distinct enough to shine through studio interference and the collective process.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Career_pathways">
        Career pathways
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Film_director&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career pathways">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="thumb tright">
       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">
        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Pedro_Almodovar_and_Pen%C3%A9lope_Cruz_2.jpg">
         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="296" data-file-width="432" height="151" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/Pedro_Almodovar_and_Pen%C3%A9lope_Cruz_2.jpg/220px-Pedro_Almodovar_and_Pen%C3%A9lope_Cruz_2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0c/Pedro_Almodovar_and_Pen%C3%A9lope_Cruz_2.jpg/330px-Pedro_Almodovar_and_Pen%C3%A9lope_Cruz_2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/0c/Pedro_Almodovar_and_Pen%C3%A9lope_Cruz_2.jpg 2x" width="220"/>
        </a>
        <div class="thumbcaption">
         <div class="magnify">
          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Pedro_Almodovar_and_Pen%C3%A9lope_Cruz_2.jpg" title="Enlarge">
          </a>
         </div>
         Director
         <a href="/wiki/Pedro_Almod%C3%B3var" title="Pedro Almodóvar">
          Pedro Almodóvar
         </a>
         and actress
         <a href="/wiki/Pen%C3%A9lope_Cruz" title="Penélope Cruz">
          Penélope Cruz
         </a>
        </div>
       </div>
      </div>
      <p>
       Some film directors started as
       <a href="/wiki/Screenwriter" title="Screenwriter">
        screenwriters
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Film_editor" title="Film editor">
        film editors
       </a>
       or actors.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">
        <a href="#cite_note-20">
         [20]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Several American cinematographers have become directors, including
       <a href="/wiki/Barry_Sonnenfeld" title="Barry Sonnenfeld">
        Barry Sonnenfeld
       </a>
       , originally the Coen brothers' DP;
       <a href="/wiki/Jan_de_Bont" title="Jan de Bont">
        Jan de Bont
       </a>
       , cinematographer on films as
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Die_Hard" title="Die Hard">
         Die Hard
        </a>
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Basic_Instinct" title="Basic Instinct">
         Basic Instinct
        </a>
       </i>
       , directed
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Speed_(1994_film)" title="Speed (1994 film)">
         Speed
        </a>
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Twister_(1996_film)" title="Twister (1996 film)">
         Twister
        </a>
       </i>
       . Recently
       <a href="/wiki/Wally_Pfister" title="Wally Pfister">
        Wally Pfister
       </a>
       , cinematographer on
       <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Nolan" title="Christopher Nolan">
        Christopher Nolan
       </a>
       's three Batman films made his directorial debut with
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Transcendence_(2014_film)" title="Transcendence (2014 film)">
         Transcendence
        </a>
       </i>
       . Other film directors have attended a
       <a href="/wiki/Film_school" title="Film school">
        film school
       </a>
       to "get formal training and education in their craft".
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">
        <a href="#cite_note-21">
         [21]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Film students generally study the basic skills used in making a film.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">
        <a href="#cite_note-22">
         [22]
        </a>
       </sup>
       This includes, for example, preparation, shot lists and storyboards, blocking, protocols of dealing with professional actors, and reading scripts.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">
        <a href="#cite_note-23">
         [23]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Some film schools are equipped with sound stages and post-production facilities.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">
        <a href="#cite_note-24">
         [24]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Besides basic technical and logistical skills, students also receive education on the nature of professional relationships that occur during film production.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">
        <a href="#cite_note-25">
         [25]
        </a>
       </sup>
       A full degree course can be designed for up to five years of studying.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">
        <a href="#cite_note-26">
         [26]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Future directors usually complete short films during their enrollment.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Princeton_18-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Princeton-18">
         [18]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_National_Film_School_of_Denmark" title="The National Film School of Denmark">
        The National Film School of Denmark
       </a>
       has the student's final projects presented on national TV.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">
        <a href="#cite_note-27">
         [27]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Some film schools retain the rights for their students' works.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">
        <a href="#cite_note-28">
         [28]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Many directors successfully prepared for making feature films by working in television.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">
        <a href="#cite_note-29">
         [29]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The
       <a href="/wiki/Deutsche_Film-_und_Fernsehakademie_Berlin" title="Deutsche Film- und Fernsehakademie Berlin">
        German Film and Television Academy Berlin
       </a>
       consequently cooperates with the Berlin/Brandenburg TV station
       <a href="/wiki/Rundfunk_Berlin-Brandenburg" title="Rundfunk Berlin-Brandenburg">
        RBB (Berlin-Brandenburg Broadcasting)
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Arte" title="Arte">
        ARTE
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">
        <a href="#cite_note-30">
         [30]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       A handful of top directors made from $133.3 million to $257.95 million in 2011, such as
       <a href="/wiki/James_Cameron" title="James Cameron">
        James Cameron
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Steven_Spielberg" title="Steven Spielberg">
        Steven Spielberg
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stephen_Chow_31-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Stephen_Chow-31">
         [31]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In May 2011, the average United States film director made $92,220.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32">
        <a href="#cite_note-32">
         [32]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
