      <p>
       A
       <b>
        cameo role
       </b>
       or
       <b>
        cameo appearance
       </b>
       (
       <span class="nowrap">
        <span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt">
         <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">
          /
          <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted">
           <span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">
            ˈ
           </span>
           <span title="'k' in 'kind'">
            k
           </span>
           <span title="/æ/: 'a' in 'bad'">
            æ
           </span>
           <span title="'m' in 'my'">
            m
           </span>
           <span title="/i/: 'y' in 'happy'">
            i
           </span>
           <span title="/oʊ/: 'o' in 'code'">
            oʊ
           </span>
          </span>
          /
         </a>
        </span>
       </span>
       ; often shortened to just
       <b>
        cameo
       </b>
       ) is a brief appearance or voice part of a known person in a work of the
       <a href="/wiki/Performing_arts" title="Performing arts">
        performing arts
       </a>
       , typically unnamed or appearing as themselves. These roles are generally small, many of them non-speaking ones, and are commonly either appearances in a work in which they hold some special significance (such as actors from an original movie appearing in its remake) or renowned people making uncredited appearances. Short appearances by
       <a href="/wiki/Celebrity" title="Celebrity">
        celebrities
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Film_director" title="Film director">
        film directors
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Politician" title="Politician">
        politicians
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Athlete" title="Athlete">
        athletes
       </a>
       , or
       <a href="/wiki/Musician" title="Musician">
        musicians
       </a>
       are common. A crew member of the movie or show playing a minor role can be referred to as a cameo as well, such as
       <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Hitchcock" title="Alfred Hitchcock">
        Alfred Hitchcock
       </a>
       's
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Alfred_Hitchcock_cameo_appearances" title="List of Alfred Hitchcock cameo appearances">
        frequently performed cameos
       </a>
       .
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Concept">
        Concept
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cameo_appearance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Concept">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Originally "cameo role" meant "a small character part that stands out from the other minor parts". The
       <a href="/wiki/Oxford_English_Dictionary" title="Oxford English Dictionary">
        Oxford English Dictionary
       </a>
       connects this with the meaning "a short literary sketch or portrait", which is based on the literal meaning of "
       <a href="/wiki/Cameo_(carving)" title="Cameo (carving)">
        cameo
       </a>
       ", a miniature carving on a gemstone.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       More recently, "cameo" has come to refer to any short appearances, whether as a character or as oneself, such as the examples below.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Cameos are generally not credited because of their brevity, or a perceived mismatch between the celebrity's stature and the film or
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TV_show" title="TV show">
        TV show
       </a>
       in which he or she is appearing. Many are
       <a href="/wiki/Publicity_stunt" title="Publicity stunt">
        publicity stunts
       </a>
       . Others are acknowledgments of an actor's contribution to an earlier work, as in the case of many
       <a href="/wiki/Film_adaptation" title="Film adaptation">
        film adaptations
       </a>
       of TV series, or of
       <a href="/wiki/Remake" title="Remake">
        remakes
       </a>
       of earlier films. Others honour artists or celebrities known for work in a particular field.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Possibly the best-known series of cameos was by
       <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Hitchcock" title="Alfred Hitchcock">
        Alfred Hitchcock
       </a>
       , who made
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Alfred_Hitchcock_cameo_appearances" title="List of Alfred Hitchcock cameo appearances">
        very brief appearances in most of his films
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Cameos also occur in
       <a href="/wiki/Novel" title="Novel">
        novels
       </a>
       and other literary works. “Literary cameos” usually involve an established character from another work who makes a brief appearance to establish a
       <a href="/wiki/Shared_universe" title="Shared universe">
        shared universe
       </a>
       setting, to make a point, or to offer
       <a href="/wiki/Homage_(arts)" title="Homage (arts)">
        homage
       </a>
       .
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Balzac" title="Balzac">
        Balzac
       </a>
       often employed this practice, as in his
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/La_Com%C3%A9die_humaine" title="La Comédie humaine">
         Comédie humaine
        </a>
       </i>
       . Sometimes a cameo features a historical person who "drops in" on fictional characters in a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Historical_novel" title="Historical novel">
        historical novel
       </a>
       , as when
       <a href="/wiki/Benjamin_Franklin" title="Benjamin Franklin">
        Benjamin Franklin
       </a>
       shares a
       <a href="/wiki/Beer" title="Beer">
        beer
       </a>
       with Phillipe Charboneau in
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Bastard_(novel)" title="The Bastard (novel)">
         The Bastard
        </a>
       </i>
       by
       <a href="/wiki/John_Jakes" title="John Jakes">
        John Jakes
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       A cameo appearance can be made by the author of a work to put a sort of personal "signature" on a story.
       <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Nabokov" title="Vladimir Nabokov">
        Vladimir Nabokov
       </a>
       often put himself in his novels; for instance, the very minor character Vivian Darkbloom (an anagram of the author's name) in
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Lolita" title="Lolita">
         Lolita
        </a>
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Straumann2008_4-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Straumann2008-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Film_directors">
        Film directors
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cameo_appearance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Film directors">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Further information:
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_directors_who_appear_in_their_own_films" title="List of directors who appear in their own films">
        List of directors who appear in their own films
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Quentin_Tarantino" title="Quentin Tarantino">
        Quentin Tarantino
       </a>
       provides cameos or
       <a href="/wiki/Bit_part" title="Bit part">
        small roles
       </a>
       in at least 10 of his movies.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Likewise,
       <a href="/wiki/Peter_Jackson" title="Peter Jackson">
        Peter Jackson
       </a>
       has made brief cameos in all of his movies, except for his first feature-length film
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Bad_Taste" title="Bad Taste">
         Bad Taste
        </a>
       </i>
       in which he plays a main character, as well as
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Hobbit:_The_Battle_of_the_Five_Armies" title="The Hobbit: The Battle of the Five Armies">
         The Battle of the Five Armies
        </a>
       </i>
       . For example, he plays a peasant eating a carrot in
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings:_The_Fellowship_of_the_Ring" title="The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring">
         The Fellowship of the Ring
        </a>
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Hobbit:_The_Desolation_of_Smaug" title="The Hobbit: The Desolation of Smaug">
         The Desolation of Smaug
        </a>
       </i>
       ; a Rohan warrior in
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings:_The_Two_Towers" title="The Lord of the Rings: The Two Towers">
         The Two Towers
        </a>
       </i>
       and a Corsair of Umbar boatswain in
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings:_The_Return_of_the_King" title="The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King">
         The Return of the King
        </a>
       </i>
       . All four were non-speaking "blink and you miss him" appearances, although in the Extended Release of
       <i>
        The Return of the King
       </i>
       , his character was given more screen time and his reprise of the carrot eating peasant in The Desolation of Smaug was featured in the foreground in reference to The Fellowship of the Ring - last seen twelve years earlier.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Director
       <a href="/wiki/Martin_Scorsese" title="Martin Scorsese">
        Martin Scorsese
       </a>
       appears in the background of his films as a bystander or an
       <a href="/wiki/Unseen_character" title="Unseen character">
        unseen character
       </a>
       . In
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Who%27s_That_Knocking_at_My_Door" title="Who's That Knocking at My Door">
         Who's That Knocking at My Door
        </a>
       </i>
       , he appears as one of the gangsters; he is a lighting crew man in
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/After_Hours_(film)" title="After Hours (film)">
         After Hours
        </a>
       </i>
       and a passenger in
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Taxi_Driver" title="Taxi Driver">
         Taxi Driver
        </a>
       </i>
       . He opens up his 1986 film
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Color_of_Money" title="The Color of Money">
         The Color of Money
        </a>
       </i>
       with a monologue on the art of playing pool. In addition, he appears with his wife and daughter as wealthy New Yorkers in
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Gangs_of_New_York" title="Gangs of New York">
         Gangs of New York
        </a>
       </i>
       , and he appears as a theatre-goer and is heard as a movie projectionist in
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Aviator_(2004_film)" title="The Aviator (2004 film)">
         The Aviator
        </a>
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In a same way,
       <a href="/wiki/Roman_Polanski" title="Roman Polanski">
        Roman Polanski
       </a>
       appears as a hired hoodlum in his film
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Chinatown_(1974_film)" title="Chinatown (1974 film)">
         Chinatown
        </a>
       </i>
       , slitting Jack Nicholson's nose with the blade of his clasp knife.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Actors_and_writers">
        Actors and writers
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cameo_appearance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Actors and writers">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Directors sometimes cast well-known lead actors with whom they have worked in the past in other films.
       <a href="/wiki/Mike_Todd" title="Mike Todd">
        Mike Todd
       </a>
       's film
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Around_the_World_in_80_Days_(1956_film)" title="Around the World in 80 Days (1956 film)">
         Around the World in 80 Days
        </a>
       </i>
       (1956) was filled with cameo roles: (
       <a href="/wiki/John_Gielgud" title="John Gielgud">
        John Gielgud
       </a>
       as an English butler,
       <a href="/wiki/Frank_Sinatra" title="Frank Sinatra">
        Frank Sinatra
       </a>
       playing piano in a saloon), and others. The stars in cameo roles were pictured in oval insets in posters for the film, and gave the term wide circulation outside the theatrical profession.
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/It%27s_a_Mad,_Mad,_Mad,_Mad_World" title="It's a Mad, Mad, Mad, Mad World">
         It's a Mad, Mad, Mad, Mad World
        </a>
       </i>
       (1963), an "epic comedy", also features cameos from nearly every popular American comedian alive at the time, including
       <a href="/wiki/The_Three_Stooges" title="The Three Stooges">
        the Three Stooges
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Jerry_Lewis" title="Jerry Lewis">
        Jerry Lewis
       </a>
       , a silent appearance by
       <a href="/wiki/Buster_Keaton" title="Buster Keaton">
        Buster Keaton
       </a>
       and a voice-only cameo by
       <a href="/wiki/Selma_Diamond" title="Selma Diamond">
        Selma Diamond
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       Among the many cameos featured in
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Maverick_(film)" title="Maverick (film)">
         Maverick
        </a>
       </i>
       , (directed by
       <a href="/wiki/Richard_Donner" title="Richard Donner">
        Richard Donner
       </a>
       ),
       <a href="/wiki/Danny_Glover" title="Danny Glover">
        Danny Glover
       </a>
       (
       <a href="/wiki/Mel_Gibson" title="Mel Gibson">
        Mel Gibson
       </a>
       's co-star in the
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lethal_Weapon_(film_series)" title="Lethal Weapon (film series)">
         Lethal Weapon
        </a>
       </i>
       <a href="/wiki/Media_franchise" title="Media franchise">
        franchise
       </a>
       of films also directed by Donner) appears as the lead
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bank_robber" title="Bank robber">
        bank robber
       </a>
       . He and Maverick (Gibson) share a scene where they look as if they knew each other, but then shake it off. As Glover makes his escape with the
       <a href="/wiki/Money" title="Money">
        money
       </a>
       , he mutters "I'm too old for this shit", his character's
       <a href="/wiki/Catchphrase" title="Catchphrase">
        catchphrase
       </a>
       in the
       <i>
        Lethal Weapon
       </i>
       film series. In addition, a strain of the main
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Theme_(music)" title="Theme (music)">
        theme
       </a>
       from
       <i>
        Lethal Weapon
       </i>
       plays in the
       <a href="/wiki/Film_score" title="Film score">
        score
       </a>
       when Glover is revealed. Actress
       <a href="/wiki/Margot_Kidder" title="Margot Kidder">
        Margot Kidder
       </a>
       made a cameo appearance in the same film as a
       <a href="/wiki/Robbery" title="Robbery">
        robbed
       </a>
       villager, and the actress previously starred as
       <a href="/wiki/Lois_Lane" title="Lois Lane">
        Lois Lane
       </a>
       in one of Donner's earlier films
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Superman_(1978_film)" title="Superman (1978 film)">
         Superman
        </a>
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RevEbert_9-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-RevEbert-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Ben_Stiller" title="Ben Stiller">
        Ben Stiller
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Vince_Vaughn" title="Vince Vaughn">
        Vince Vaughn
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Owen_Wilson" title="Owen Wilson">
        Owen Wilson
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Luke_Wilson" title="Luke Wilson">
        Luke Wilson
       </a>
       , and
       <a href="/wiki/Will_Ferrell" title="Will Ferrell">
        Will Ferrell
       </a>
       have made appearances in so many of the same films (whether as lead characters or cameos)
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/USA_Today" title="USA Today">
         USA Today
        </a>
       </i>
       coined the term the "
       <a href="/wiki/Frat_Pack" title="Frat Pack">
        Frat Pack
       </a>
       " to name the group.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">
        <a href="#cite_note-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Actor
       <a href="/wiki/Adam_Sandler" title="Adam Sandler">
        Adam Sandler
       </a>
       is also known for frequently casting fellow
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">
         Saturday Night Live
        </a>
       </i>
       performers (including
       <a href="/wiki/Rob_Schneider" title="Rob Schneider">
        Rob Schneider
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/David_Spade" title="David Spade">
        David Spade
       </a>
       ) in various roles in his films (as well as making cameo appearances of his own in theirs, most of which he co-produces).
       <a href="/wiki/Sam_Raimi" title="Sam Raimi">
        Sam Raimi
       </a>
       frequently uses his brother
       <a href="/wiki/Ted_Raimi" title="Ted Raimi">
        Ted
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Campbell" title="Bruce Campbell">
        Bruce Campbell
       </a>
       in his films.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The American singer/actress
       <a href="/wiki/Cher" title="Cher">
        Cher
       </a>
       has done a couple of cameos over the years. She did two cameos in
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Will_%26_Grace" title="Will &amp; Grace">
         Will &amp; Grace
        </a>
       </i>
       and she even did a few in the 90s.
      </p>
      <p>
       The animated series
       <i>
        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adventures_of_Tintin" title="Adventures of Tintin">
         Adventures of Tintin
        </a>
       </i>
       featured its author
       <a href="/wiki/Herg%C3%A9" title="Hergé">
        Hergé
       </a>
       in all the episodes.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">
        <a href="#cite_note-13">
         [13]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Stan_Lee" title="Stan Lee">
        Stan Lee
       </a>
       is well known for his cameo appearances throughout most of the
       <a href="/wiki/List_of_films_based_on_Marvel_Comics" title="List of films based on Marvel Comics">
        Marvel films
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">
        <a href="#cite_note-14">
         [14]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/An_Adventure_in_Space_and_Time" title="An Adventure in Space and Time">
         An Adventure in Space and Time
        </a>
       </i>
       , a drama about how the
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Who" title="Doctor Who">
         Doctor Who
        </a>
       </i>
       began, features many
       <a href="/wiki/An_Adventure_in_Space_and_Time#Cast" title="An Adventure in Space and Time">
        actors from the show's past
       </a>
       , including two past companions in a party scene, another as a mother calling her children in for dinner and a fourth in a car park at the BBC.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">
        <a href="#cite_note-15">
         [15]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h3>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Real-life_people">
        Real-life people
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cameo_appearance&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Real-life people">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h3>
      <p>
       Films based on actual events occasionally include cameo
       <a href="/wiki/Guest_appearance" title="Guest appearance">
        guest appearances
       </a>
       by the people portrayed in them. In
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/The_Pursuit_of_Happyness" title="The Pursuit of Happyness">
         The Pursuit of Happyness
        </a>
       </i>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Chris_Gardner" title="Chris Gardner">
        Chris Gardner
       </a>
       makes a cameo at the end.
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/24_Hour_Party_People" title="24 Hour Party People">
         24 Hour Party People
        </a>
       </i>
       , a film about
       <a href="/wiki/Tony_Wilson" title="Tony Wilson">
        Tony Wilson
       </a>
       , has a cameo by the real Tony Wilson and many other notable people. In the film
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Apollo_13_(film)" title="Apollo 13 (film)">
         Apollo 13
        </a>
       </i>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Jim_Lovell" title="Jim Lovell">
        James Lovell
       </a>
       (the real commander of that flight) and his wife Marilyn appear next to the actors playing them (
       <a href="/wiki/Tom_Hanks" title="Tom Hanks">
        Tom Hanks
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Kathleen_Quinlan" title="Kathleen Quinlan">
        Kathleen Quinlan
       </a>
       respectively).
       <a href="/wiki/Domino_Harvey" title="Domino Harvey">
        Domino Harvey
       </a>
       makes a short appearance in the credits of
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Domino_(2005_film)" title="Domino (2005 film)">
         Domino
        </a>
       </i>
       , while the real
       <a href="/wiki/Erin_Brockovich" title="Erin Brockovich">
        Erin Brockovich
       </a>
       has a cameo appearance as a waitress named Julia in the
       <a href="/wiki/Erin_Brockovich_(film)" title="Erin Brockovich (film)">
        eponymous movie
       </a>
       (where her role is played by actress
       <a href="/wiki/Julia_Roberts" title="Julia Roberts">
        Julia Roberts
       </a>
       ).
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">
          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">
           citation needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       In a
       <a href="/wiki/Flashback_(narrative)" title="Flashback (narrative)">
        flashback
       </a>
       sequence in
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Fear_and_Loathing_in_Las_Vegas_(film)" title="Fear and Loathing in Las Vegas (film)">
         Fear and Loathing in Las Vegas
        </a>
       </i>
       , Raoul Duke (played by
       <a href="/wiki/Johnny_Depp" title="Johnny Depp">
        Johnny Depp
       </a>
       ) runs into the real life
       <a href="/wiki/Hunter_S._Thompson" title="Hunter S. Thompson">
        Hunter S. Thompson
       </a>
       , upon whom the character of Duke is based, leading him to remark "There I was...mother of God, there I am! Holy fuck."
      </p>
      <p>
       <a href="/wiki/Elon_Musk" title="Elon Musk">
        Elon Musk
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Larry_Ellison" title="Larry Ellison">
        Larry Ellison
       </a>
       , both founders of large technology companies, are featured in cameos in the 2010 blockbuster,
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Iron_Man_2" title="Iron Man 2">
         Iron Man 2
        </a>
       </i>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">
        <a href="#cite_note-16">
         [16]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The king of Sweden,
       <a href="/wiki/Carl_XVI_Gustaf_of_Sweden" title="Carl XVI Gustaf of Sweden">
        Carl XVI Gustaf
       </a>
       , was in the children's program "Mika"
       <i>
        (Mika och renen Ossian på äventyr)
       </i>
       when Mika was in
       <a href="/wiki/Stockholm" title="Stockholm">
        Stockholm
       </a>
       with his reindeer.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">
        <a href="#cite_note-17">
         [17]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
