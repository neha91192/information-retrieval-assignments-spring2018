      <p>
       A
       <b>
        make-up artist
       </b>
       or
       <b>
        makeup artist
       </b>
       is an artist whose medium is the human body, applying
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Makeup" title="Makeup">
        makeup
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Prosthetic_makeup" title="Prosthetic makeup">
        prosthetics
       </a>
       for
       <a href="/wiki/Theatre" title="Theatre">
        theatrical
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">
        television
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">
        film
       </a>
       , fashion, magazines and other similar productions including all aspects of the modeling industry. Awards given for this profession in the entertainment industry include the
       <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Makeup_and_Hairstyling" title="Academy Award for Best Makeup and Hairstyling">
        Academy Award for Best Makeup and Hairstyling
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and even several entertainment industry awards such as the
       <a href="/wiki/Emmy_Award" title="Emmy Award">
        Emmy Awards
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and the
       <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" title="Golden Globe Award">
        Golden Globes
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In the United States as well as the other parts of the globe, professional licenses are required by agencies in order for them to hire the MUA. Bigger production companies
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       have in-house makeup artists on their payroll although most MUA’s generally are freelance
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       and their times remain flexible depending on the projects.The use of digital cameras may have made the use of bridal make up more popular.
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Makeup_techniques">
        Makeup techniques
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Make-up_artist&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Makeup techniques">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <dl>
       <dt>
        Fashion
       </dt>
      </dl>
      <p>
       Fashion makeup is used in magazine photography as well as on the fashion runway.
       <a href="/wiki/Avant-garde" title="Avant-garde">
        Avant-garde
       </a>
       makeup is also an applicable technique used for projects that require experimental themes. Fashion makeup is also commonly used in
       <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">
        television
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">
        film
       </a>
       ranging for the natural
       <i>
        prime look
       </i>
       to more sophisticated applications such as
       <i>
        color balance
       </i>
       .
      </p>
      <dl>
       <dt>
        Theatrical makeup
       </dt>
      </dl>
      <p>
       Stage makeup is used as a method in conjunction with stage lighting to highlight the actors' faces in order to make expressions visible to the audience from moderate distances. This often includes defining the eyes and lips as well as the highlights and lowlights of the facial bones.
      </p>
      <dl>
       <dt>
        Special make-up effects (FX makeup)
       </dt>
      </dl>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Prosthetic_makeup" title="Prosthetic makeup">
        Prosthetic makeup
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       The use of special effects techniques enhancing physical features to exhibit
       <span class="cleanup-needed-content" style="padding-left:0.1em; padding-right:0.1em; color:#595959; border:1px solid #DDD;">
        <a href="/wiki/Metaphysics" title="Metaphysics">
         metaphysical
        </a>
        characteristics
       </span>
       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">
        [
        <i>
         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify">
          <span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (January 2016)">
           clarification needed
          </span>
         </a>
        </i>
        ]
       </sup>
       as well as fantasy makeup. The use of prosthetics and plaster casting are also required for projects that entails non-human appearances. Accents such as
       <a href="/wiki/Theatrical_blood" title="Theatrical blood">
        theatrical blood
       </a>
       and ooze are also techniques applicable to this type of makeup.
      </p>
      <dl>
       <dt>
        Airbrushing
       </dt>
      </dl>
      <p>
       The use of an
       <a href="/wiki/Airbrush" title="Airbrush">
        airbrush
       </a>
       which is a small air-operated device that sprays various media including alcohol and water-based makeup by a process of nebulization. The earliest record of this type of cosmetic application dates back to the 1925
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">
        <a href="#cite_note-7">
         [7]
        </a>
       </sup>
       film version of Ben-Hur, it has recently been re-popularized by the advent of HDTV and digital photography, where the camera focuses on higher depths of detail. Liquid foundations that are high in coverage but thin in consistency are applied with the airbrush for full coverage without a heavy build-up of product.
      </p>
      <dl>
       <dt>
        Bridal makeup
       </dt>
      </dl>
      <p>
       Bridal makeup is a new segment in a makeup artist's repertoire. From ethnic, to glamorous, to contemporary, makeup artists are now an important part of wedding planning in Asia, Europe, Africa and North America.
      </p>
      <dl>
       <dt>
        High definition
       </dt>
      </dl>
      <p>
       This is an art which involves the use of light
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">
        <a href="#cite_note-8">
         [8]
        </a>
       </sup>
       reflectors and ingredients such as minerals to give the skin a flawless finish. This was developed due to the further development of
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HDTV" title="HDTV">
        high definition
       </a>
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">
        <a href="#cite_note-9">
         [9]
        </a>
       </sup>
       mediums and the cost implications of airbrush makeup.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Makeup_artists_in_Bollywood">
        Makeup artists in Bollywood
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Make-up_artist&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Makeup artists in Bollywood">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In 1955 the
       <a href="/wiki/Bollywood" title="Bollywood">
        Bollywood
       </a>
       group Cine Costume Make-Up Artist &amp; Hair Dressers' Association (CCMAA) created a rule that did not allow women to obtain memberships as makeup artists.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraphindia1_10-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-telegraphindia1-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       However, in 2014 the
       <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_India" title="Supreme Court of India">
        Supreme Court of India
       </a>
       ruled that this rule was in violation of the Indian constitutional guarantees granted under Article 14 (right to equality), 19(1)(g) (freedom to carry out any profession) and Article 21 (right to liberty).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraphindia1_10-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-telegraphindia1-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The judges of the Supreme Court of India stated that the ban on women makeup artist members had no "rationale nexus" to the cause sought to be achieved and was "unacceptable, impermissible and inconsistent" with the constitutional rights guaranteed to the citizens.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraphindia1_10-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-telegraphindia1-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       The Court also found illegal the rule which mandated that for any artist, female or male, to work in the industry, they must have domicile status of five years in the state where they intend to work.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-telegraphindia1_10-3">
        <a href="#cite_note-telegraphindia1-10">
         [10]
        </a>
       </sup>
       In 2015 it was announced that Charu Khurana had become the first woman to be registered by the Cine Costume Make-Up Artist &amp; Hair Dressers' Association.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">
        <a href="#cite_note-11">
         [11]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       In June 2014, the Cine Costume Make-Up Artist &amp; Hair Dressers' Association (CCMAA) authorised an official protest on the movie set of
       <a href="/wiki/Bang_Bang!" title="Bang Bang!">
        Bang Bang!
       </a>
       in protest of a foreign makeup artist, Daniel Bauer working on the movie for its lead actress,
       <a href="/wiki/Katrina_Kaif" title="Katrina Kaif">
        Katrina Kaif
       </a>
       . The CCMAA and 15 of its members protested on the movie set as Daniel Bauer was not registered with the Union, despite the Union banning foreign artists working in Bollywood. The issue was resolved with the CCMAA granting Daniel Bauer full membership
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">
        <a href="#cite_note-12">
         [12]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
