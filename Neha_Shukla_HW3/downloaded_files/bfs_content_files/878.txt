      <p>
       <b>
        Pre-production
       </b>
       is the process of fixing some of the elements involved in a
       <a href="/wiki/Film" title="Film">
        film
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Play_(theatre)" title="Play (theatre)">
        play
       </a>
       , or other
       <a href="/wiki/Performance" title="Performance">
        performance
       </a>
       . There are three parts in a production: pre-production, production, and post-production. Pre-production ends when the planning ends and the content starts being produced.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="In_film">
        In film
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pre-production&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: In film">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">
       Main article:
       <a href="/wiki/Filmmaking#Pre-production" title="Filmmaking">
        Filmmaking § Pre-production
       </a>
      </div>
      <p>
       In
       <a href="/wiki/Filmmaking" title="Filmmaking">
        filmmaking
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Video_production" title="Video production">
        video production
       </a>
       , pre-production formally begins once a project has been
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greenlight" title="Greenlight">
        greenlit
       </a>
       . At this stage, finalizing preparations for
       <a href="/wiki/Filmmaking#Production" title="Filmmaking">
        production
       </a>
       go into effect. Financing will generally be confirmed and many of the key elements such as principal
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cast_member" title="Cast member">
        cast members
       </a>
       , director and
       <a href="/wiki/Cinematographer" title="Cinematographer">
        cinematographer
       </a>
       are set. By the end of pre-production, the
       <a href="/wiki/Screenplay" title="Screenplay">
        screenplay
       </a>
       is usually finalized and satisfactory to all the financiers and other stakeholders.
      </p>
      <p>
       During pre-production, the
       <a href="/wiki/Screenplay" title="Screenplay">
        script
       </a>
       is broken down into individual scenes
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Storyboards" title="Storyboards">
        storyboards
       </a>
       and all the locations, props, cast members, costumes, special effects and visual effects are identified. An extremely detailed
       <i>
        schedule
       </i>
       is produced and arrangements are made for the necessary elements to be available to the film-makers at the appropriate times. Sets are constructed, the crew is hired, financial arrangements are put in place and a start date for the beginning of
       <a href="/wiki/Principal_photography" title="Principal photography">
        principal photography
       </a>
       is set. At some point in pre-production there will be a
       <a href="/wiki/Read-through" title="Read-through">
        read-through
       </a>
       of the script which is usually attended by all cast members with speaking parts, the director, all heads of departments, financiers, producers, and publicists.
      </p>
      <p>
       Even though the writer may still be working on it, the
       <a href="/wiki/Screenplay" title="Screenplay">
        screenplay
       </a>
       is generally
       <i>
        page-locked
       </i>
       and
       <i>
        scene-numbered
       </i>
       at the beginning of pre-production to avoid confusion. This means that even though additions and deletions may still be made, any particular scene will always fall on the same page and have the same scene number.
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="In_music">
        In music
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pre-production&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: In music">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       In the
       <a href="/wiki/Music_industry" title="Music industry">
        music industry
       </a>
       , pre-production is a process whereby a recording artist spends time creating and refining their musical ideas. The artist thus produces a song's
       <a href="/wiki/Demo_(music)" title="Demo (music)">
        demo recording
       </a>
       , or rough draft, in order to pre-establish the song's creative premise. This reduces the time and money spent in expensive studios. The goal is to enter into the major recording phase of production with the basic and most promising ideas having been already established.
      </p>
      <p>
       Notable producers who preferred this process have included
       <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Fairbairn" title="Bruce Fairbairn">
        Bruce Fairbairn
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Bob_Rock" title="Bob Rock">
        Bob Rock
       </a>
       . They have both produced successful albums such as
       <a href="/wiki/Bon_Jovi" title="Bon Jovi">
        Bon Jovi
       </a>
       's
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Slippery_When_Wet" title="Slippery When Wet">
         Slippery When Wet
        </a>
       </i>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/M%C3%B6tley_Cr%C3%BCe" title="Mötley Crüe">
        Mötley Crüe
       </a>
       's
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Dr._Feelgood_(album)" title="Dr. Feelgood (album)">
         Dr. Feelgood
        </a>
       </i>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Metallica" title="Metallica">
        Metallica
       </a>
       's
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Metallica_(album)" title="Metallica (album)">
         The Black Album
        </a>
       </i>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Aerosmith" title="Aerosmith">
        Aerosmith
       </a>
       's
       <i>
        <a href="/wiki/Permanent_Vacation_(album)" title="Permanent Vacation (album)">
         Permanent Vacation
        </a>
       </i>
       .
      </p>
