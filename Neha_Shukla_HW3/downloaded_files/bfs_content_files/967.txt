   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">
    Line producer
   </h1>
   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">
    <div class="noprint" id="siteSub">

    </div>
    <div id="contentSub">
    </div>

    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">
     <div class="mw-parser-output">
      <p>
       A
       <b>
        line producer
       </b>
       is a type of
       <a href="/wiki/Film_producer" title="Film producer">
        film producer
       </a>
       who is the key manager during daily operations of a
       <a href="/wiki/Feature_film" title="Feature film">
        feature film
       </a>
       ,
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Television_advertisement" title="Television advertisement">
        advertisement film
       </a>
       ,
       <a href="/wiki/Television_film" title="Television film">
        television film
       </a>
       , or an
       <a href="/wiki/Episode" title="Episode">
        episode
       </a>
       of a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TV_program" title="TV program">
        TV program
       </a>
       . A line producer works on one film at a time.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Film_Connection_Film_Institute_1-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Film_Connection_Film_Institute-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
       They are responsible for
       <a href="/wiki/Human_resources" title="Human resources">
        human resources
       </a>
       and handling any problems that come up during production.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-2">
         [2]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
      </p>

      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Responsibilities">
        Responsibilities
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Line_producer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Responsibilities">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       According to
       <a href="/wiki/Producers_Guild_of_America" title="Producers Guild of America">
        Producers Guild of America
       </a>
       (PGA) guidelines, the Line Producer is the individual who reports directly to the individual(s) receiving "Produced By" credit on the theatrical motion picture and is the single individual who has the primary responsibility for the logistics of the production, from pre-production through completion of production; all Department Heads report to the Line Producer.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Discovery_3-0">
        <a href="#cite_note-Discovery-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <p>
       The line producer functions like a
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chief_operations_officer" title="Chief operations officer">
        chief operations officer
       </a>
       in running the production company. During pre-production, responsibilities include supervising the assembly of the shooting company, recruitment of key personnel and services, and production organization for how to shoot the script and transform it into a movie. The line producer plans start dates for everyone and everything, and monitors the budget in the lead up to picture. Film production generally follows a rigorous schedule. The line producer facilitates casting,
       <a href="/wiki/Location_scouting" title="Location scouting">
        location scouting
       </a>
       , set building and decorating, offices and stages, wardrobe, props, stunts, physical and visual effects, camera, lighting, rigging, transportation, cast, crew and union relations, travel, cast and crew accommodation, contracting of legal permissions and agreements, safety and risk management, prep and shooting schedule. In short, the line producer oversees the joint planning, negotiations, implementation and accounting for production.
      </p>
      <p>
       In the studio system, the line producer reports to the studio and typically liaises with key executives of production divisions inside the studio such as physical production, legal, labor relations, insurance, and finance. The line producer supports the
       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Director_(film)" title="Director (film)">
        director
       </a>
       's vision and direct influence on the creative expression or narrative of the film,
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">
        <a href="#cite_note-4">
         [4]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Though it could be argued that, through a line producer's ability to influence aspects of the film, like allocation of resources to certain departments, they can change important aspects of the film that have creative consequences, e.g., production value. For example, they can affect the project's look by influencing the choice of
       <a href="/wiki/Filming_location" title="Filming location">
        filming locations
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Discovery_3-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Discovery-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
       While the director is in charge of all purely artistic decisions, the line producer help substantiate the director's creative ideas by taking care of
       <a href="/wiki/Logistics" title="Logistics">
        logistics
       </a>
       and related issues.
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">
        <a href="#cite_note-5">
         [5]
        </a>
       </sup>
       From
       <a href="/wiki/Pre-production" title="Pre-production">
        pre-production
       </a>
       through
       <a href="/wiki/Principal_photography" title="Principal photography">
        principal photography
       </a>
       , the line producer oversees the
       <a href="/wiki/Production_budget" title="Production budget">
        production budget
       </a>
       and physical needs of the shoot. By the first day of production, several versions of the budget have usually been drafted. A finalized or "locked" budget is the basis for the production to move forward. A key objective of a line producer is to respect this "locked" budget and deliver in time.
      </p>
      <p>
       While in production, the line producer oversees the execution of many decisions that must be made to deliver each day's shoot. The administrative aspects, especially those that have any financial impact, are all crucial areas of the line producer's work. These areas include but are not limited to negotiating compensation (usually during pre-production) of crew members (both for union and non-union productions) and resolving daily production issues (in conjunction with the
       <a href="/wiki/Assistant_director" title="Assistant director">
        first assistant director
       </a>
       and possibly the
       <a href="/wiki/Unit_production_manager" title="Unit production manager">
        unit production manager
       </a>
       ).
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">
        <a href="#cite_note-6">
         [6]
        </a>
       </sup>
       Moreover, they provide demanded equipment. If required, they handle unanticipated scheduling changes and serve "as a liaison between the crew and the producer."
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Discovery_3-2">
        <a href="#cite_note-Discovery-3">
         [3]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
      <h2>
       <span class="mw-headline" id="Becoming_a_line_producer">
        Becoming a line producer
       </span>
       <span class="mw-editsection">
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         [
        </span>
        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Line_producer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Becoming a line producer">
         edit
        </a>
        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">
         ]
        </span>
       </span>
      </h2>
      <p>
       Line producers usually come from the ranks of assistant directors and unit production managers, giving them a strong background in the logistics of
       <a href="/wiki/Filmmaking" title="Filmmaking">
        filmmaking
       </a>
       and
       <a href="/wiki/Time_management" title="Time management">
        time management
       </a>
       . It is common for them to continue to perform in one of those roles on projects they line produce. They do not necessarily attend a
       <a href="/wiki/Film_school" title="Film school">
        film school
       </a>
       .
       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Film_Connection_Film_Institute_1-1">
        <a href="#cite_note-Film_Connection_Film_Institute-1">
         [1]
        </a>
       </sup>
      </p>
